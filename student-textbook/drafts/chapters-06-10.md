# Chapter 6 - Recurrent Stability and Temporal Presence

**Opening puzzle.** Imagine two devices that receive the same brief signal. In Device R, the signal enters a network whose later activity feeds back into earlier processing. If the network is nudged away from its current state, it tends to return toward a content-specific pattern before eventually moving on. In Device F, the signal passes through a one-way chain of slow components. The activity lasts just as long, and a graph of average activation looks nearly identical, but nothing later makes a causal difference to anything earlier. If both devices retain the signal for 240 milliseconds, have they achieved the same kind of temporal organization?

The question matters because a duration measurement cannot, by itself, tell us how a state lasts. Cø / N* proposes that minimal phenomenal presence requires more than an instantaneous feedforward event. Its third network condition, $N_3$, concerns bounded recurrent stability: content-relevant activity is maintained through return relations or an explicitly defended metastable surrogate, long enough to be present but not defined as permanently fixed. The theory therefore takes a risk. A slow feedforward process that matches every valid consciousness-related anchor would challenge the claim that recurrent topology is necessary.

**Why it matters.** Timing is often treated as if “later” meant “more conscious.” That shortcut confuses at least four things: initial encoding, recurrent stabilization, working-memory maintenance, and preparation for report. This chapter gives you a vocabulary and a research strategy for keeping them apart.

## Chapter map

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- explain the difference between recurrence, persistence, a passive trace, and working-memory maintenance;
- interpret the bounded-dwell condition without treating it as a universal stopwatch;
- distinguish temporal presence from the temporal character of an experience;
- design a perturbation that targets $N_3$ without simply erasing the content or disrupting report;
- compare the Cø / N* proposal fairly with Recurrent Processing Theory and duration-only rivals; and
- state what evidence would support, weaken, or leave the recurrence claim unresolved.

The central discipline is simple to state and hard to practice: do not infer a causal loop from a long-lasting signal, and do not infer phenomenal timing from the timing of a button press.

## 1. A state can last in more than one way

The notation $N_3(S,c)$ says that candidate system $S$ has the required temporal organization for content $c$. The content index matters. A brain, robot, or organoid can contain oscillations and feedback everywhere while the particular content under study passes through only once. Global recurrence is not automatically content-relevant recurrence.

Four temporal patterns should be separated.

1. A **feedforward duration** occurs when activity travels in one causal direction but moves through slow units or long delays.
2. **Recurrent processing** occurs when an earlier content-supporting state makes a difference to a later state through a directed return relation in a time-unrolled causal model.
3. A **passive trace** persists in a sensor, receptor, buffer, or slow variable without continuing to shape the active content-supporting process.
4. **Working-memory maintenance** keeps information available for later use, often through executive control, task demands, or rehearsal that may extend beyond the proposed interval of phenomenal presence.

These patterns can overlap in real systems. The scientific problem is not to force every episode into a single box. It is to determine which causal organization explains the measured persistence and which part of that organization the theory claims is necessary.

**Plain language.** A state can remain on the screen because the program keeps repainting it, because the pixels fade slowly, because a copy is stored in memory, or because a user keeps pressing “refresh.” The visible duration may be the same while the sustaining process differs.

**Analogy: the echoing room and the long hallway.** In an echoing room, sound returns after reflecting from the walls. In a long hallway lined with open doors, a sound can arrive at successive locations over time without ever returning. A microphone at the far end might record activity for the same total duration in both cases. The echoing room illustrates a return relation; the hallway illustrates extended feedforward propagation.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Neural or artificial recurrence is not literal acoustic reflection. A causal return can transform, gate, or reconstruct a content rather than replay it. Moreover, an echo may be behaviorally irrelevant, whereas $N_3$ concerns a return that helps maintain a content-bearing organization. The analogy distinguishes topology, not consciousness.

To establish recurrence, researchers need evidence about causal dependence. In a time-unrolled graph, a state at time $t_1$ influences a later state at $t_2$, and some pathway from later processing returns influence to a stage or variable that helps determine the content at $t_3$. A genuine self-feedback relation may qualify if intervening on the earlier state changes the later content beyond what continuing input and passive decay predict. A familiar oscillation or high autocorrelation is only a candidate measure until such dependence is established.

**Checkpoint.** For any temporal measure you encounter, ask two questions: “What makes the state last?” and “What shows that the lasting activity still concerns the same content?”

## 2. Bounded dwell and the idea of metastability

The core temporal proposal can be written:

$$
\exists L\subseteq S\quad\text{such that}\quad
t_{\min}\leq \operatorname{dwell}(c;L)\leq t_{\max}.
$$

Here $L$ is a content-supporting subnetwork within the candidate system $S$. The expression $\operatorname{dwell}(c;L)$ is the time during which the relevant content remains in a recurrently or metastably maintained organization. The lower bound $t_{\min}$ excludes an evanescent event that never stabilizes. The upper bound $t_{\max}$ resists the assumption that indefinite persistence is always “more conscious.” Together the bounds aim at **metastability**: enough stability to sustain an episode, with enough flexibility for updating, transition, and decay.

The bounds are operational hypotheses, not constants discovered once for every species, modality, and substrate. A visual event in an adult human, an olfactory state in an octopus, and an artificial control state may operate on different time scales. A serious study must freeze the relevant window before examining the decisive result and justify why that window tracks the proposed temporal role.

**Do not infer.** Exceeding a proposed upper bound does not automatically prove unconsciousness. A seizure-like fixation, a deeply absorbed state, or an unusually long pain episode may reveal that the bound measures healthy flexibility, a particular estimator’s validity range, or one biological implementation rather than minimal presence itself. The theory owes a revision if valid countercases accumulate.

**Analogy: balancing a bicycle.** A moving bicycle is stable without being locked in place. Small corrections keep it within a viable range, yet it remains able to turn. A bicycle welded upright is more fixed but not more dynamically stable in the relevant sense. This captures the contrast between metastable maintenance and inert persistence.

**Where the analogy breaks.** A bicycle’s stability is a whole-vehicle control property, while $N_3$ is indexed to a particular content. The analogy also says nothing about integration, system-wide availability, or phenomenal evidence. It helps explain why “lasting longer” and “being dynamically maintained” are not synonyms.

Candidate estimators include survival time within a preregistered trajectory region, return toward a content-specific attractor after a small perturbation, recurrent effective connectivity, a hidden-state plateau, and pathway-dependent recovery. Each estimator carries assumptions. Autocorrelation may confuse recurrence with slow decay. Effective-connectivity estimates may depend heavily on model choice. Attractor language may oversimplify trajectories that are metastable without settling into a fixed basin. Therefore, multiple estimators should be compared, and credible disagreement should produce an indeterminate component assessment rather than a convenient average.

**Method note.** Metastable return without an identifiable loop may be evaluated as an alternative $N_3$ surrogate, but it must not be silently treated as evidence for the stronger recurrent-topology claim. Investigators must freeze the surrogate's causal role, intervention signature, and agreement rule in advance. If a loop-based estimator and a metastability estimator disagree beyond the declared tolerance, the $N_3$ result is conflicted or indeterminate; the favorable measure cannot simply replace the other after inspection.

Onset and offset signals require the same role analysis. Late activity may reflect report or memory, while absence of one familiar late component does not establish absence of every recurrent route. If loop-based and metastable-surrogate estimators disagree beyond tolerance, return a conflicted or indeterminate assessment rather than selecting the favorable estimator after inspection.

**Source note.** The bounded-dwell formulation and the recurrence-versus-persistence distinction are developed in “Cø as N*” and audited in “Ablating N*: Does Every Conjunct Earn Its Place?”. The source manuscripts present the bounds as testable operational commitments, not established universal constants.

## 3. Timing the intervention without changing the question

A perturbation can be technically successful and inferentially useless. Suppose content identity first becomes measurable around 60 milliseconds, candidate recurrent stabilization occupies approximately 110-220 milliseconds, working-memory maintenance begins around 260 milliseconds, and report preparation begins around 340 milliseconds. An intervention delivered at 50 milliseconds may prevent the content from forming. An intervention delivered at 370 milliseconds may disrupt only the report. Neither result selectively tests whether recurrence maintained phenomenal presence.

A licensed $N_3$ perturbation should target a return pathway or its effective gain during a preregistered stabilization interval. It should preserve:

- initial content fidelity;
- viability $V$;
- integration $N_1$;
- report-independent availability $N_2$;
- the autonomy and cross-condition identity of the candidate referent; and
- the operating regime in which the measures are valid.

“Preserve” requires both that the full paired-change interval for every retained component lie within its preregistered equivalence band and that each post-intervention lower bound remain at or above its gate. After the perturbation, the positive-bearer set must be recomputed. A smaller or alternative candidate may now satisfy the model, or bearer attribution may become unresolved. The study should not hold positive-bearer membership fixed as part of the intervention license, because that would protect the outcome the theory is supposed to predict.

**Method note.** The cleanest design often has at least five conditions: an early encoding disruption, a targeted recurrent-path disruption, a late report or memory disruption, a sham intervention, and a selective rescue. The controls help identify what the target manipulation did; the rescue tests whether restoring the proposed causal role restores the predicted anchor pattern.

### Thought experiment: The five-pulse theater

A research team can deliver one magnetic pulse during a visual trial. It considers five timings:

- Pulse E occurs before content identity can be decoded.
- Pulse T occurs during the proposed recurrent stabilization interval.
- Pulse L occurs after working memory begins but before overt report.
- Pulse R occurs during response preparation.
- Pulse S is a sham that produces the same sound and expectation without neural perturbation.

The team observes that E, T, and R all reduce verbal accuracy. T also reduces a no-report physiological discrimination marker; E reduces early content fidelity; R leaves the no-report marker intact.

**Stage 1.** Draw a causal timeline and state which timing most directly addresses $N_3$.

**Stage 2.** List the measurements needed to show that the target timing preserved $N_1$, $N_2$, viability, and candidate identity.

**Stage 3.** Design a rescue manipulation. What must recover, and what nonspecific recovery would fail to support the recurrence claim?

**Stage 4.** Identify at least two ways the same observations could still be explained by a duration-only model.

The point is not to guess the intended answer. It is to discover how many assumptions stand between “a pulse changed behavior” and “recurrent stability is necessary for phenomenal presence.”

## 4. Temporal presence is not temporal character

$N_3$ asks whether a content is maintained through an appropriate interval. It does not yet say how the experience unfolds within that interval. The character extension uses a temporal path profile, written $\Pi_c$, for features such as direction, rate, curvature, order, dwell pattern, and hysteresis - path dependence in which the current state depends on how it was reached - in a quality structure.

Two episodes can both pass $N_3$ while differing markedly in temporal character. A pain may be steady or throbbing. A melody may rise or fall. Apparent motion may feel smooth or stuttering. A color may fade evenly or flicker. These differences concern the path an admitted content traces, not whether the content crosses the presence gate at all.

**Plain language.** $N_3$ asks whether there is a sustained episode. $\Pi_c$ asks what the episode’s temporal shape is like.

### Thought experiment: The frozen chord and the moving phrase

System A sustains a chord through a stable recurrent pattern for 600 milliseconds. System B traverses a sequence of related pitch states over the same interval, also through recurrent processing. Both meet the same lower and upper dwell bounds and retain equivalent integration and availability.

**Stage 1.** Explain why matching $N_3$ values would not imply matching experiences.

**Stage 2.** Propose relational features that could distinguish a held chord from an unfolding phrase without using the participant’s final report as the presence criterion.

**Stage 3.** Imagine a perturbation that reverses the order of transitions while preserving the presence gate. What independent phenomenal relation should a character theory predict will change?

**Stage 4.** Suppose the perturbation changes the neural trajectory but no validated phenomenal relation. Which claim is threatened: the presence gate, the temporal-character bridge, both, or neither? Defend the scope of your answer.

This separation makes the research program modular. Failure of a proposed path-to-character mapping does not automatically show that the episode lacked presence. Conversely, a richly structured trajectory does not prove presence if the gate is negative, indeterminate, or unlicensed.

## 5. Rival lenses: recurrence, duration, and access

Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT) is the nearest neighboring family. RPT emphasizes feedback within sensory processing and argues, in at least some versions, that local recurrent processing is central or sufficient for perceptual consciousness. Cø / N* accepts the pressure behind this view: a single feedforward sweep may be too thin to constitute temporally present content. It differs by placing recurrence inside a conjunction. A local loop that is not irreducibly integrated or cannot causally reach functionally diverse recipients fails $N_1$ or $N_2$, even if it is recurrent.

Global Workspace and Global Neuronal Workspace approaches often associate conscious access with widespread availability and later ignition-like dynamics. Cø / N* overlaps at $N_2$ but refuses to use report, executive control, or a single late signature as the minimal criterion. Its $N_3$ interval may occur before working-memory and response processes that workspace experiments often measure. Exact contrasts depend on the dated version of each rival; neither “RPT” nor “workspace theory” is one unchanging prediction rule.

Duration-only rivals claim that what matters is sufficiently persistent internal state, not loop topology. They become especially important for artificial systems that use delays, state-space memory, or long time constants without an obvious recurrent graph. If such a system matches all independently validated anchors, survives intervention tests, and transports across contexts, the recurrence requirement should be weakened or rejected unless loop topology makes a further risky prediction.

**Do not infer.** Showing that biological brains normally use recurrence would not establish recurrence as a substrate-general necessity. It may identify a common implementation. Necessity requires diagnostic cases in which recurrence and duration come apart.

The fairest comparison therefore uses systems or interventions that match dwell time while varying return topology, or match topology while varying content-relevant causal influence. Rival models should be frozen before the decisive data are inspected. If one model is allowed to redefine recurrence after every counterexample while another is held fixed, the comparison is not adversarial.

## 6. Evidence, limits, and direct challenges

Evidence favors the $N_3$ claim when a content-relevant return path predicts more than duration alone, a selective disruption crosses the $N_3$ gate while retained conditions remain equivalent, independent anchors change as preregistered, and a role-specific rescue restores those anchors. Convergent estimators should agree on the intermediate temporal role, and the effect should survive reasonable changes in boundary, grain, timing, and preprocessing.

Evidence directly challenges the claim when:

- a well-supported conscious content occurs under genuine absence of feedback;
- a valid, selective $N_3$ ablation leaves every independent phenomenal anchor unchanged;
- feedforward systems with matched duration predict all held-out and intervention results as well as recurrent models;
- the upper dwell bound systematically excludes credible conscious episodes; or
- recurrence estimates track report, arousal, or generic slowness rather than the target content.

Other outcomes are less decisive. A global anesthetic that reduces recurrence, integration, availability, behavior, and data quality at once does not identify $N_3$ specifically. A null effect from an underpowered perturbation is indeterminate. A study that cannot preserve the candidate boundary or operating regime is unlicensed for the selective claim. These categories are not rhetorical shelters; they specify different failures and different next experiments.

**Method note.** Write the strongest conclusion the design earns, then remove any word that implies a broader domain, stronger kind of necessity, or more selective intervention than the evidence supports.

**Source note.** The separation of early encoding, recurrent stabilization, working memory, and report is developed in “Cø as N*” and “Ablating N*: Does Every Conjunct Earn Its Place?”. The contrast with RPT is a family-level orientation; an actual experiment must freeze an exact rival version.

## Chapter summary

$N_3$ proposes that minimal phenomenal presence requires content-relevant maintenance through bounded recurrent or defensibly metastable dynamics. Duration alone cannot identify recurrence; late activity can reflect memory or report; and a passive trace can persist without continuing causal influence. The dwell bounds aim at flexible stability, not a universal stopwatch. Selective tests must target the proposed stabilization interval, preserve other components and candidate identity, and recompute the bearer outcome. The recurrence claim gains force only where topology and duration come apart. Temporal presence is also distinct from temporal character: an episode can remain present while its experienced path changes.

## Key terms

- **Bounded dwell:** maintenance of a content between preregistered lower and upper temporal bounds.
- **Causal recurrence:** a directed return relation through which an earlier content-supporting state affects a later state beyond continuing input and passive decay.
- **Feedforward duration:** extended activity without a causal return path.
- **Metastability:** temporary stability that remains capable of transition and decay.
- **Passive trace:** stored or slowly decaying information that does not actively maintain the content-bearing process.
- **Rescue design:** restoration of a targeted causal role after disruption to test whether the predicted anchor pattern returns.
- **Temporal character $\Pi_c$:** the direction, rate, ordering, curvature, dwell pattern, and related features of how an admitted experience unfolds.
- **Working-memory maintenance:** continued usability of information for later tasks, often involving executive or report-related processes.

# Chapter 7 - What Does the Evidence License?

**Opening puzzle.** Two assessments produce the same score: $.72$. The positive threshold is $.70$. In the first assessment, the measure has been validated for the patient’s injury class, the sensory route is intact, data-quality checks pass, and two partly independent measures agree. In the second, the measure was calibrated only in healthy adults, the patient has unresolved hearing loss, muscle activity contaminates the signal, and a credible imaging measure points in the opposite direction. Should the identical numbers receive identical interpretations?

If a test is treated as a score-producing machine, the answer may seem obvious: both pass. If a test is treated as an inference whose validity conditions can fail, the answer changes. A number has evidential meaning only within a declared target, system, method, model, and validity envelope. The Cø / N* research program therefore uses four scientific outcomes - positive, negative, indeterminate, and unlicensed - and checks applicability and quality before interpreting evidential strength.

**Plain language.** The state of a system and the state of our evidence are different things. A system may be conscious or nonconscious when our present assessment cannot responsibly settle the matter.

## Chapter map

This chapter develops an inference-control framework that applies both within Cø / N* and across rival theories. You will learn to:

- index a conclusion to a complete assessment tuple;
- apply the precedence rule before reading a score;
- distinguish positive, negative, indeterminate, and unlicensed outcomes;
- explain why positive and negative licenses are asymmetric;
- diagnose unresolved cases with a five-part cause profile;
- report coverage and unresolved-output rates rather than accuracy alone; and
- keep scientific classification separate from precautionary action.

The framework does not claim that consciousness comes in four metaphysical kinds. It says that scientific attributions require more than a binary button.

## 1. The assessment tuple prevents conclusions from drifting

Write an assessment as:

$$
\mathcal A=\langle S,C,T,E,M,\mathcal V\rangle.
$$

$S$ is the candidate system. $C$ is the target form of consciousness - for example, minimal phenomenal presence during a specified episode rather than wakefulness, reportability, or rich self-awareness. $T$ is the test or battery. $E$ is the available evidence. $M$ is the inferential model connecting evidence to the target. $\mathcal V$ is the **validity envelope**: the populations, substrates, tasks, boundaries, recording conditions, and auxiliary capacities for which that interpretation has support.

The tuple matters because conclusions tend to expand in ordinary language. Evidence of one moment of command following can be redescribed as continuous rich awareness. A signal measured in cortex can be treated as a verdict about a whole brain-body system. A measure validated in adult mammalian brains can be exported to an artificial network because its numerical range looks familiar. Each step changes one element of $\mathcal A$ without announcing the change.

**Why it matters.** A precise answer to the wrong assessment question is not a precise answer to the right one. Indexing the claim makes target substitutions visible.

**Analogy: a passport at the wrong border.** A passport can be genuine and still fail to license entry if it is expired, belongs to someone else, or is presented in a jurisdiction that requires a different document. The problem is not that the ink is weak. The document lacks the required relation to this person, place, and time. Similarly, a neural score can be measured accurately while lacking a validated interpretation for the present system or target.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Legal validity is created by institutional rules, whereas scientific validity is earned through evidence, calibration, causal understanding, and transport. A border officer may have final authority; no consciousness researcher has comparable authority over nature. The analogy concerns conditional license, not conventional truth.

The validity envelope should be prospective and inspectable. It records whether the required sensory route was available, whether the candidate boundary is stable, whether the procedure transfers to the substrate, whether auxiliary capacities such as comprehension were needed, and whether the model was validated at the chosen grain. A study conducted outside the envelope may still generate useful data. What it cannot do is inherit the original interpretation without a transport argument.

**Source note.** *Indeterminacy as a Scientific Result* introduces the assessment tuple to prevent target, system, and validity conditions from being silently widened after a result is known.

## 2. Four outcomes and a precedence rule

The decision rule $D(\mathcal A)$ can be expressed as five ordered branches:

1. **Unlicensed** if $a=0$ or $q=0$.
2. **Indeterminate** if $a=1,q=1,c=1$.
3. **Positive** if $a=1,q=1,c=0$ and $\operatorname{LB}(L)\geq\tau_+$.
4. **Negative** if $a=1,q=1,c=0$ and $\operatorname{UB}(L)<\tau_-$.
5. **Indeterminate** otherwise.

Here $a$ means applicability, $q$ means execution and data quality, $c$ means unresolved conflict among credible surrogates, and $L$ is a validated evidence grade or score. $\operatorname{LB}(L)$ and $\operatorname{UB}(L)$ are lower and upper uncertainty bounds. $\tau_+$ is the rule-in threshold; $\tau_-$ is the rule-out threshold. Because the positive rule uses $\geq$ and the negative rule uses $<$, equality at $\tau_+$ passes the rule-in boundary, whereas equality at $\tau_-$ does not license rule-out.

**Method note.** This interval rule is a conservative teaching extension, not a quotation of a universal source formula. The source framework can be written with a scalar score and calibrated rule-in and rule-out thresholds. Here a positive requires the lower uncertainty bound to meet the rule-in threshold, while a negative requires the upper uncertainty bound to fall strictly below the rule-out threshold. An empirical protocol must justify and preregister its own interval construction and equality rules rather than inherit these classroom conventions automatically.

The order is the intellectual heart of the rule. First ask whether the assessment applies. Next ask whether it was executed adequately. Then ask whether credible surrogate measures conflict. Model-family and boundary disagreement are reported separately through $O_M$ and $O_B$, rather than folded into the scalar conflict flag $c$. Only after those checks may the score enter a threshold rule. A high number does not repair failed applicability or poor data quality.

The outcomes mean:

- **Positive:** applicable, adequately executed, nonconflicted evidence licenses attribution of the specified target under the declared model and envelope.
- **Negative:** an applicable and sufficiently sensitive assessment had a fair detection opportunity and entered a validated rule-out region.
- **Indeterminate:** the assessment is interpretable and informative but does not discriminate enough, either because it lies in a gray zone or because credible evidence remains unresolved.
- **Unlicensed:** a condition required to interpret the assessment failed. The output concerns the procedure’s inferential status, not the system’s consciousness.

**Do not infer.** “Unlicensed” does not mean “probably negative,” and “indeterminate” does not mean “partly conscious.” Both are evidence states. They require different repairs.

An unlicensed result can sometimes be quarantined. If one measure in a preregistered battery fails quality checks, remove it only according to the prospective rule. If the remaining independently validated measures still form a complete battery, they may yield their own outcome. If the failed measure was the sole estimator of a required component, the bundle remains unlicensed. Analysts may not discover this dependence after seeing which removal gives the preferred verdict.

**Analogy: a courtroom with admissibility and weight.** A judge first decides whether evidence is admissible; a jury then decides how much weight admitted evidence deserves. A vivid but illegally obtained or misidentified item does not become admissible because it strongly favors one side. Likewise, applicability and quality precede evidential strength.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Scientific inference has no sharp separation between judge and jury, and validity is often graded rather than settled by a single ruling. Moreover, courts answer legal questions under institutional standards, while science revises its standards through evidence. The analogy illuminates precedence, not a complete philosophy of evidence.

## 3. Positive and negative conclusions have different burdens

The **Positive License Principle** requires that a marker’s target relevance and specificity be validated, the assessment apply, quality be adequate, and credible nonconscious or artifactual explanations be controlled. Complexity, flexible behavior, recurrent activity, self-report, or widespread activation may be evidence in a suitable design. None is a default positive simply because it sounds consciousness-related.

The **Negative License Principle** adds a different burden. A nondetection supports absence only when the indicator would probably have been detectable if the target were present, the necessary auxiliary capacities were intact or controlled, the sensory and motor routes were fair, the procedure applied to this system, and alternative causes of nondetection were excluded. A test can have strong positive specificity while having poor sensitivity for negatives.

Consider a command-following task. A reliable, correctly timed response may strongly support that the person understood and followed the command. Failure to respond could result from absence of consciousness, but also from hearing loss, language impairment, fatigue, motor paralysis, memory failure, fluctuating arousal, or an unsuitable imagery instruction. The positive and negative outcomes are not mirror images.

**Checkpoint.** Before calling a result negative, complete the sentence: “If the target were present, this procedure would probably have detected it because …” If that sentence cannot be supported, the result is not yet a licensed rule-out.

### Thought experiment: The silent diver

A deep-sea diver communicates through a hand-squeeze code. During an emergency, the diver stops squeezing. A monitor shows no hand movement, but the suit’s audio system may have failed and one glove is mechanically jammed.

**Stage 1.** Specify the target claim. Is it hearing the command, understanding it, intending to respond, being conscious, or all four?

**Stage 2.** Identify which parts of the assessment tuple are unstable.

**Stage 3.** Describe a result that would be indeterminate and one that would be unlicensed.

**Stage 4.** Design a low-demand alternative route and state what must be validated before it can support a negative conclusion.

The diver case makes a general point: a missing output can be evidence about consciousness only through a chain of auxiliary conditions. Making those conditions explicit is not special pleading. It is the difference between detecting absence and failing to detect.

## 4. Cause profiles make unresolved results productive

A bare “uncertain” result does little scientific work. The framework therefore attaches a cause profile:

$$
P_R(\mathcal A)=\langle e,s,m,b,t\rangle.
$$

$e$ is **evidential scarcity**: valid data are too imprecise or sparse. $s$ is **surrogate discordance**: credible indicators conflict. $m$ is **model dependence**: live inferential models yield different classifications. $b$ is **boundary or grain sensitivity**: reasonable candidate definitions reverse the result. $t$ is **transport uncertainty**: the evidence-to-target relation may not carry to a new population or substrate.

These entries diagnose the inference, not dimensions of experience. A case with high boundary sensitivity is not an experience with “more boundary.” It is a case whose classification changes when reasonable system definitions change.

**Notation warning.** Symbols in this research program are locally scoped. In this profile, $s$ means surrogate discordance and $m$ means model dependence. Elsewhere, the same letters may denote an availability selectivity threshold or a minimum recipient count. Define local notation when it first appears instead of assuming global meanings.

Each cause suggests a different response:

- Scarcity calls for more power, better precision, broader temporal sampling, or a more sensitive measure.
- Discordance calls for construct decomposition, replication, causal adjudication, or investigation of auxiliary demands.
- Model dependence calls for a diagnostic prediction or an explicit justification for weighting models.
- Boundary sensitivity calls for consciousness-independent individuation and boundary perturbation.
- Transport uncertainty calls for construct, causal-role, calibration, boundary, and regime preservation tests.

This yields a useful asymmetry. Indeterminate evidence may become decisive through more informative evidence. An unlicensed assessment must first be repaired. Repeating an invalid test with more trials can produce a highly reproducible nonanswer.

### Thought experiment: The translation machine

A neural measure predicts reports in awake adult humans with strong held-out accuracy. A team applies the same numerical pipeline to a synthetic agent built from unfamiliar components. The agent’s score falls deep in the human positive range. The team has no evidence that the measured feature plays the same causal role, that the candidate boundary is comparable, or that the agent’s internal timing lies within the measure’s validity range.

**Stage 0.** Before inspecting the agent score, state the exact source-domain claim being transported: target, candidate, construct, calibration population, validity envelope, thresholds, and strongest licensed conclusion. A number cannot be transported responsibly until the interpretation attached to it has been frozen.

**Stage 1.** Classify the human result and the agent result separately. Do not assume that a shared number has a shared meaning.

**Stage 2.** Build a cause profile for the agent assessment.

**Stage 3.** Propose a staged transport program. Which evidence would test construct preservation, causal-role preservation, calibration, and boundary/regime preservation?

**Stage 4.** Suppose the team has ethical reasons to avoid harming the agent during validation. Explain how a precautionary policy could be justified without changing the scientific status.

## 5. Model and boundary disagreement are results, not noise

Suppose several live inferential models are frozen in advance. We can display their outputs as:

$$
O_M(\mathcal A)=\{D_m(\mathcal A):m\in\mathfrak M\},
$$

where $\mathfrak M$ is the declared model family. Similarly, for a mapped family of reasonable candidate boundaries $\mathfrak B$:

$$
O_B(\mathcal A)=\{D(\mathcal A;B):B\in\mathfrak B\}.
$$

If these sets contain more than one outcome, comparative model or boundary indeterminacy remains even when each individual analysis is internally decisive. The sets are not probability distributions. They are transparent displays of how conclusions vary across declared alternatives. Analysts must not remove a model or boundary after learning that it produces an inconvenient result.

This makes the framework theory-plural without pretending to be assumption-free. Integrated Information Theory, workspace theories, recurrent-processing theories, higher-order theories, and Cø / N* can assign different roles to the same observation. Their disagreements become scientifically useful when they yield frozen, diagnostic predictions. Merely counting theories or averaging their conclusions can hide rather than resolve the conflict.

**Method note.** Record the version, date, target, and operational commitments of every rival. “Workspace theory predicts X” is not sufficiently precise when different workspace formulations disagree about report, broadcasting, anatomy, or timing.

The four-outcome framework can itself fail. If trained reviewers cannot reliably distinguish indeterminate from unlicensed cases, if the extra category improves neither calibration nor error detection, if unresolved outputs absorb adverse findings only after inspection, or if protocol burden exceeds epistemic benefit, the framework needs revision. A preregistered **failure budget** can specify the unresolved-output rate or pattern that triggers methodological repair.

## 6. Coverage, selective risk, and the temptation to hide hard cases

An assessment should report more than accuracy among the cases it classifies. **Coverage** is the proportion receiving positive or negative outcomes. Let $r_I$ be the indeterminate rate and $r_U$ the unlicensed rate. **Selective risk** is the error rate among classified cases, when a defensible independent reference exists.

Suppose Tool A attempts 100 assessments and returns 30 positive, 20 negative, 25 indeterminate, and 25 unlicensed. Coverage is $.50$, $r_I=.25$, and $r_U=.25$. If five of the 50 classified cases disagree with a defensible reference, selective risk is $.10$. Tool B classifies 20 easy cases with no observed errors and abstains or fails on the remaining 80. Its measured selective risk is lower, but its coverage is only $.20$. It is not automatically the better general tool.

**Why it matters.** A test can look excellent by avoiding difficult cases. Coverage and unresolved-output rates reveal where its apparent accuracy comes from.

Confidence intervals are also needed. An unresolved rate based on 12 cases is not the same evidential object as the same rate based on 1,200 cases. Outcomes should be stratified by population, boundary, severity, and transport domain, because a single average may hide systematic failure in precisely the cases for which the test is needed.

## 7. Scientific status and practical action must remain separate

Let $D$ be the scientific status and let $\Lambda$ represent a loss structure: the costs of false denial, false attribution, delay, burden, and irreversible action. A practical decision uses both. High stakes, asymmetrical errors, and a low-cost reversible option can justify precaution under indeterminacy.

For example, imagine an indeterminate patient assessment. A short, low-burden alternative communication trial costs little. Missing covert command following would be serious. Trying the communication route can be rational even though the scientific classification remains indeterminate. Conversely, a high-risk intervention with low expected benefit may not be justified by the same evidence.

**Do not infer.** Precaution does not transform $D=\text{Indeterminate}$ into $D=\text{Positive}$. The practical policy is a conclusion from evidence plus values and consequences, not a hidden consciousness verdict.

This distinction matters in animal welfare, organoid research, artificial systems, and clinical care. It allows protective action without corrupting the scientific record. It also prevents a negative practical recommendation from masquerading as evidence of absence.

**Source note.** *Indeterminacy as a Scientific Result* treats science-action separation as a guardrail. The paper’s contribution is methodological; it does not supply calibrated probabilities or one universal policy threshold.

## 8. Evidence, limits, and rival approaches

Three-way decision methods and classifiers with a reject option already show that abstention can improve error control. The four-outcome framework adds a distinction between valid nondiscrimination and failed interpretability. A reject option usually assumes the classifier applies and declines because confidence is low; that resembles indeterminate. Unlicensed covers cases in which the score’s intended interpretation has not been established.

Bayesian approaches can represent uncertainty continuously, but a precise posterior can still rest on a failed target, boundary, likelihood, or transport assumption. Probabilities do not remove the need for applicability checks. Conversely, threshold categories should not erase useful probability distributions. The approaches can be combined: report calibrated uncertainty within the licensed part of the model, along with categorical status and cause profile.

Evidence supports this framework if independent reviewers apply it reliably, cause profiles predict efficient repair strategies, coverage and unresolved rates improve calibration and transparency, and preregistration reduces opportunistic reclassification. Evidence weakens it if the categories cannot be distinguished, add no predictive or practical value, or become asymmetrical escape routes for favored theories.

The framework’s strongest protection against misuse is prospective commitment. Freeze applicability criteria, quality rules, conflict handling, thresholds, model and boundary families, quarantine rules, and failure budgets before inspecting critical outcomes. When a result is adverse, the response should be the one already attached to that cause - not an improvised new category.

## Chapter summary

Scientific classification should follow inferential license rather than numerical appearance. The assessment tuple fixes the system, target, test, evidence, model, and validity envelope. Applicability and quality precede conflict handling; conflict precedes thresholds. Positive, negative, indeterminate, and unlicensed are evidential outcomes, not amounts of experience. Positive and negative claims have asymmetric burdens, and unresolved cases should receive cause profiles that direct distinct repairs. Coverage and unresolved-output rates reveal what accuracy alone can hide. Practical action adds stakes and error costs but must not rewrite the scientific result.

## Key terms

- **Assessment tuple $\mathcal A$:** the indexed system, target, test, evidence, model, and validity envelope.
- **Cause profile:** a record of evidential scarcity, surrogate discordance, model dependence, boundary sensitivity, and transport uncertainty.
- **Coverage:** the proportion of attempted cases receiving positive or negative classifications.
- **Indeterminate:** a valid, informative assessment that does not discriminate sufficiently.
- **Negative License Principle:** nondetection supports absence only under validated sensitivity and a fair detection opportunity.
- **Positive License Principle:** an impressive marker supports attribution only when relevance, specificity, applicability, and quality are established.
- **Selective risk:** error among the cases a test chooses to classify.
- **Unlicensed:** a meta-output indicating that conditions required to interpret the assessment failed.
- **Validity envelope:** the domain in which the evidence-to-target interpretation is supported.

# Chapter 8 - Does Every Conjunct Earn Its Place?

**Opening puzzle.** A security system opens a vault only when four indicators are positive: electricity is available, the keycard is valid, the fingerprint matches, and the code is correct. In every recorded success, all four indicators are positive. In every recorded failure, all four are negative because the building lost power during emergency drills. The four-indicator rule predicts the dataset perfectly. So does the keycard alone. So does the fingerprint alone. So do many two-indicator rules. Has the dataset shown that each indicator is necessary?

The Cø / N* theory has the same structural problem. Its profile rule is conjunctive: viability $V$, integration $N_1$, report-independent system-wide availability $N_2$, and recurrent stability $N_3$ must all pass. A conjunction advertises more than a useful bundle-level association. It says every term is needed. Yet data in which the components rise and fall together cannot identify the necessity of any particular term.

**Why it matters.** Writing “and” between four variables is easy. Showing that nature requires every “and” is a separate research program.

## Chapter map

This chapter teaches you to:

- distinguish logical, incremental empirical, and causal necessity;
- derive the exact cases on which a full conjunction and a leave-one-out rival disagree;
- explain why diagnostic coverage is a result rather than a nuisance;
- separate criterion, mechanism, and measurement ablations;
- apply an anti-circularity firewall when no consciousness oracle exists;
- propagate component rules through the candidate lattice before making bearer-level claims; and
- classify an audit as indispensable, redundant, causally coupled, indeterminate, or unlicensed.

The main lesson is diagnostic: a model can predict ordinary cases well while its internal factorization remains almost completely untested.

## 1. Three different meanings of “necessary”

The word *necessary* can name three claims.

**Logical necessity** means a term appears in the written formula. If the rule is $A\land B$, then the formula returns false when $A$ is false. Inspection of the syntax establishes this. It tells us what the theory says, not whether the world requires $A$.

**Incremental empirical necessity** means the full model predicts independent held-out anchors better than a declared rival that omits the term, specifically in cases where the two rules disagree. This conclusion is relative to the anchor family, population, operationalization, and rival class.

**Causal necessity** means selectively changing the term’s physical realization changes independent anchors while the retained components, candidate referent, and operating regime remain equivalent. This is stronger than criterion comparison, but it remains relative to the mechanism and domain tested.

**Plain language.** A component can be required by a spreadsheet formula, improve predictions in a dataset, and play a causal role in a physical system. Those are three achievements, not three ways of saying the same thing.

**Analogy: ingredients in a recipe.** A printed bread recipe may list salt. Removing the word “salt” changes the recipe. A taste test may show that the original predicts preferred loaves better than a no-salt recipe. A controlled baking experiment may show that salt changes gluten behavior while flour, water, yeast, temperature, and proofing are held stable. These correspond roughly to logical inclusion, empirical value, and causal contribution.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Recipe goals are chosen and multidimensional; phenomenal presence is not a flavor score. Ingredients also interact, and removing one can change the whole process - precisely the problem that makes selective mechanism ablation difficult. The analogy clarifies levels of necessity but does not provide a consciousness anchor.

This distinction prevents common overclaims. A lesion that reduces behavior does not automatically show causal necessity for phenomenal presence. A leave-one-out model that predicts worse does not show metaphysical necessity against every imaginable theory. And a component’s appearance in the definition cannot be cited as empirical evidence for its own indispensability.

**Source note.** “Ablating N*: Does Every Conjunct Earn Its Place?” develops this three-way distinction and treats component necessity as a held-out, anchor-relative audit rather than an automatic consequence of the conjunction.

## 2. The diagnostic-set result

For case $x$, write the component vector as:

$$
X(x)=(X_0,X_1,X_2,X_3)=(V,N_1,N_2,N_3).
$$

The full profile rule is:

$$
M_*(x)=\bigwedge_{j=0}^{3}X_j(x),
$$

meaning that every component must pass. The rule with component $k$ removed is:

$$
M_{-k}(x)=\bigwedge_{j\neq k}X_j(x).
$$

The two rules disagree only in the diagnostic set:

$$
\mathcal D_k=
\left\{x:X_k(x)=0\land\bigwedge_{j\neq k}X_j(x)=1\right\}.
$$

Read the formula in words: the omitted component fails while every retained component passes. Why is this the only disagreement cell? If the full conjunction passes, then the ablated conjunction must pass because it asks for fewer things. If any retained component fails, both rules fail. Only a single failure in the omitted term lets the ablated rule pass while the full rule fails.

For $N_2$, the exact profile is $(V,N_1,N_2,N_3)=(1,1,0,1)$. Cases at $(1,1,1,1)$ cannot distinguish the rules because both predict positive. Cases at $(0,0,0,0)$ cannot distinguish them because both predict negative. More examples from those corners increase precision about bundle-level prediction but add no direct information about whether $N_2$ earns its place. For the deterministic Boolean profile rules just defined, a study containing no members of $\mathcal D_k$ gives the two rules identical casewise predictions. Any apparent performance difference must then arise from additional estimation, weighting, or modeling choices that require separate justification.

**Checkpoint.** Before comparing two models, write the set of cases on which their predictions differ. If the study contains none, a performance tie is guaranteed and a performance “win” usually comes from something other than the declared rules.

**Analogy: testing bridge supports only on calm days.** Suppose a bridge has four support systems. Engineers inspect it only when every support is intact or when a flood disables all four. Those observations may show that the complete bridge works in normal conditions and fails in catastrophe. They do not show what each support contributes. To test Support 3, engineers need cases where Support 3 changes while the others remain functional.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Ethical engineering does not require damaging a bridge with people on it, and naturally occurring near-misses may substitute for intervention. Consciousness audits face an additional difficulty: there is no universally accepted direct outcome measure. The analogy explains diagnostic variation, not how to construct independent anchors.

Empirical component estimates have uncertainty. A conservative diagnostic region requires the upper bound for the omitted component to fall below its threshold and the lower bounds for retained components to clear theirs. A point estimate just below threshold does not qualify if its interval straddles. The **diagnostic margin** is the distance to the nearest relevant threshold. Larger margins reduce ambiguity about membership, although they do not guarantee that the case is representative.

## 3. Coverage determines the scope of the result

Let $\Gamma_k$ denote profile-level diagnostic coverage: the target-distribution weight in the region where the full and ablated rules disagree. An engineered case can produce a dramatic full-model advantage while representing only a tiny fraction of the intended population. The local result may be real, but the broad claim remains weak.

Consider a study of $N_2$ with threshold $.60$. One case has an $N_2$ upper bound of $.42$, while lower bounds for $V$, $N_1$, and $N_3$ are $.73$, $.80$, and $.69$. Its nearest-threshold margin is:

$$
m_2=\min(.60-.42,\ .73-.60,\ .80-.60,\ .69-.60)=.09.
$$

This is a conservative $N_2$ diagnostic case. Suppose the full model outperforms the ablated rival on held-out anchors, but diagnostic cases carry only five percent of the population weight. The study supports local incremental value. It does not establish that $N_2$ frequently discriminates the models across the population.

Coverage also has a bearer-level counterpart. The profile rules operate on each frozen candidate referent, but the theory’s final positive subject attribution uses a candidate lattice and inclusion-minimality. For model $m$:

$$
\mathcal B_m^+(c)=
\operatorname{Min}_{\subseteq}
\{S\in\mathcal P_\Theta:M_m(S,c)=1\}.
$$

$\mathcal P_\Theta$ is the frozen family of causally admissible candidates under specification $\Theta$. The operator $\operatorname{Min}_{\subseteq}$ retains candidates that have no qualifying all-pass proper subsystem. A diagnostic case somewhere in the lattice is necessary for the full and ablated bearer sets to differ, but it is not sufficient. The diagnostic candidate may be nonminimal under both rules. Removing one component can also make a smaller candidate pass, causing a larger candidate to disappear from the ablated minimal set.

**Do not infer.** A profile-level component result is not automatically a complete claim about where the subject is. Recompute the candidate lattice under every rival, then apply the same mapping, stability, and separability licenses.

This distinction prevents two opposite errors. Freezing the original positive bearer after ablation protects the theory from relocation or loss. Treating every all-pass candidate as a subject ignores minimality. The fair comparison freezes candidate generation and rules but allows each model’s bearer output to change.

## 4. An anti-circularity firewall without a consciousness oracle

The audit cannot define “conscious” as “passes $V\land N^*$” and then show that the full model predicts those labels better than an ablated rival. That would be syntactic self-confirmation. Instead, separate two evidence streams.

The **component stream** $X$ estimates $V$, $N_1$, $N_2$, and $N_3$. The **anchor stream** $Y$ contains independently constructed evidence that may include calibrated reports in appropriate cases, low-demand discrimination, metacognitive structure, physiological relations, perturbational signatures validated outside the critical comparison, and theory-light clinical outcomes. No anchor is an infallible consciousness oracle. The aim is comparative prediction across partially independent evidence.

The firewall requires:

1. treating the identity claim as a hypothesis, never a database definition;
2. separating component construction from anchor construction;
3. splitting development, calibration, and final adjudication data;
4. prohibiting one measure from constructing and validating the same component in the same fold;
5. freezing boundary family, grain, content, regime, interval, thresholds, interventions, anchor battery, translation rule, scoring rule, and reasonable-specification family; and
6. comparing full and ablated models on held-out diagnostic cases while recomputing each bearer set.

**Method note.** Draw a lineage diagram for every variable. If two differently named quantities share the same sensor, preprocessing step, learned labels, or selection rule, declare the overlap, run a leave-channel-out analysis, and downgrade the conclusion if it does not survive.

### Thought experiment: The circular scholarship test

A university defines “excellent scholarship” as having four features: clarity, novelty, rigor, and impact. It trains a model to score the features. The final excellence label in its database is then set to 1 exactly when all four scores pass. Researchers remove the novelty term and report that prediction of “excellent scholarship” becomes worse.

**Stage 1.** Identify the circular step.

**Stage 2.** Design an anchor vector that preserves disagreement and is not constructed from the four feature scores.

**Stage 3.** Specify development, calibration, and adjudication splits.

**Stage 4.** Explain why even a clean held-out advantage would remain relative to the chosen anchors rather than proving the true essence of scholarship.

The consciousness audit has the same logic but higher stakes. Independence does not provide certainty. It provides a comparison that can genuinely go against the theory.

## 5. Three ablations ask three questions

The word *ablation* often hides important differences.

A **criterion ablation** removes a term from the decision rule. It asks whether the term adds held-out predictive discrimination. No physical system must be damaged.

A **mechanism ablation** changes a proposed physical realization. For a target in $\{N_1,N_2,N_3\}$, it asks about causal contribution only if the manipulation selectively changes that target while preserving retained components, viability, referent identity, and mapping. A $V$-targeted mechanism audit requires the separate logic stated below.

A **measurement ablation** removes a sensor, estimator, feature family, or preprocessing route. It asks whether the component classification is robust to measurement choices. It says nothing directly about what would happen if the physical component were deleted.

These operations can yield different results. A component may be empirically indispensable while no selective physical intervention is currently possible. A mechanism intervention may change several components together, leading to a causally coupled status. A measurement route may be removable without showing that the underlying component is redundant.

For an intervention $\iota_k$ intended to reduce component $k$, define a matched-control change vector:

$$
\boldsymbol\delta(\iota_k)=
(\Delta s_0,\Delta s_1,\Delta s_2,\Delta s_3),
$$

where the four scores correspond to $(V,N_1,N_2,N_3)$. For $k\in\{1,2,3\}$, a selectivity license requires the full uncertainty interval for the target reduction to meet the preregistered direction and magnitude, $\Delta s_k\leq-\eta_k$; every retained change interval must lie inside its equivalence band; every retained post-intervention lower bound must still clear its gate; and $V$ must remain above its gate. The validity of the measurements and the frozen candidate referent's autonomy and cross-condition mapping must also remain licensed. Positive-bearer membership is deliberately not a condition of selectivity; it is an outcome to recompute.

A $V$-targeted audit must distinguish two questions that the phrase "viability remains licensed" can otherwise blur. The first is whether the $V$ gate passes; the second is whether the $V$ estimator, candidate mapping, anchor battery, and operating-regime interpretation remain valid. In a diagnostic $V$ case, the gate may fail while those assessment licenses remain intact. If reducing $V$ also destroys the validity envelope or makes the referent unmappable, the mechanism result is unlicensed rather than evidence that $V$ is causally necessary. Criterion-level comparison of the full rule with a $V$-ablated rival remains possible on independently valid cases, but it does not inherit causal status from the label "viability intervention."

For a preregistered scalar summary $g(Y)$ of the independent anchors, $\psi_k$ is the target-versus-control anchor contrast among licensed diagnostic cases. It is an evidential-anchor contrast, not an average treatment effect on directly observed consciousness.

**Plain language.** Calling an intervention “the recurrence lesion” does not prove that it changed only recurrence. Names describe intentions. Measurements and uncertainty intervals establish selectivity.

### Thought experiment: The disappearing orchestra

An engineer mutes a feedback channel in a musical robot to test $N_3$. After the change, recurrence falls, but so do integration, power stability, content fidelity, and communication among modules. The original candidate boundary also becomes unstable because two modules operate independently.

**Stage 1.** Classify the intervention as selective, coupled, or unlicensed for a causal $N_3$ claim, and list the evidence needed for your classification.

**Stage 2.** Separate what the manipulation may show about the physical mechanism from what it can show about phenomenal presence.

**Stage 3.** Redesign the intervention or propose a criterion-level audit that does not require physical selectivity.

**Stage 4.** Describe the possible post-intervention bearer outputs: loss, replacement, plurality, or unresolved attribution.

## 6. Held-out risk and five audit outcomes

Let $R_k(m)$ be the held-out risk of model $m$ on the independent anchor pattern for diagnostic cases. Lower risk is better. Define the incremental value of component $k$ as:

$$
\Delta_k=R_k(-k)-R_k(*).
$$

A positive $\Delta_k$ means the ablated model has higher risk; a negative value favors removal. But a point estimate is not a verdict. Before declaring a component indispensable, the lower uncertainty bound should exceed a preregistered meaningful-effect margin $\delta_k^{\min}$, diagnostic coverage must be adequate, flexibility must be matched, evidence streams must be independent, and reasonable specifications should yield a stable conclusion.

The audit returns five possible statuses:

- **Indispensable:** adequate diagnostic coverage and a stable, meaningful held-out advantage; a selective intervention is additionally required for a causal-necessity claim.
- **Redundant:** adequate valid diagnostic coverage exists; the upper uncertainty bound for $\Delta_k$ lies below $\delta_k^{\min}$; and the ablated rival transports at least as well under matched flexibility and without compensatory retuning.
- **Causally coupled:** a valid intervention cannot move the target component without moving retained components.
- **Indeterminate:** coverage is weak, intervals straddle, anchors conflict, or rivals tie.
- **Unlicensed:** boundary, viability, measurement, intervention, independence, or transport fails.

“Causally coupled” is not a consolation prize. It may reveal that the proposed factorization separates variables that cannot be independently realized in the tested system. That can motivate a combined component, a new intervention, or a change in the claim from universal factorization to biological implementation.

Measurement-route robustness, $\Omega_k$, summarizes how often a component status survives the removal of plausible sensors or analysis routes. Positive-specification support, $\Pi_k^+$, summarizes how often the lower risk-advantage bound clears the meaningful-effect criterion across a frozen family of boundaries, thresholds, causal metrics, and preprocessing choices. Neither frequency is a posterior probability of metaphysical necessity. Always report the distribution and the choices that reverse status.

## 7. Grouped ablations and strong minimality

One-at-a-time deletion tests weak minimality. Components may be jointly redundant or interact. For any nonempty removal set $A$:

$$
M_{-A}(x)=\bigwedge_{j\notin A}X_j(x),
$$

and the grouped diagnostic set is:

$$
\mathcal D_A=
\left\{x:
\bigwedge_{j\notin A}X_j(x)=1
\land
\bigvee_{k\in A}X_k(x)=0
\right\}.
$$

The full and grouped rivals disagree when every retained term passes and at least one removed term fails. With four terms there are $2^4-1=15$ nonempty removal sets. Testing every set raises multiplicity and feasibility problems, so a mature program should prioritize scientifically motivated single deletions and coupled sets, then expand toward all proper submodels.

Strong empirical minimality means the full model outperforms every preregistered proper submodel under matched conditions. Even that would not establish sufficiency against unrelated nonconjunctive theories. A latent composite of integration and recurrence, for example, is not simply a grouped deletion; it is a revised factorization that deserves its own predictions.

Selection into diagnostic cases can introduce bias. If measurement error or a common cause affects both component scores and anchors, choosing cases because one score is low can enrich misleading anchor patterns. Safeguards include defining diagnostic membership without viewing held-out anchors, cross-fitting, negative controls, probabilistic membership near thresholds, and unconditional sensitivity analyses.

**Do not infer.** A rare synthetic near-miss may be excellent for construct separation while saying little about biological prevalence. Diagnostic informativeness and representativeness are distinct virtues.

## 8. Evidence, limits, and rival methodologies

The component audit resembles model selection, adversarial collaboration, causal intervention, and robustness analysis, but it combines them around a conjunctive identity claim. Ordinary lesion logic often asks whether a performance drops. The present audit asks whether the manipulated component was selectively changed, whether independent anchors behaved as predicted, and whether the candidate bearer was recomputed. Virtual lesions in artificial systems can help establish engineering separability, but they do not provide consciousness ground truth.

Evidence favors a conjunct when diagnostic cases exist, the full model gains stable held-out value over matched rivals, selective interventions and rescues produce the preregistered anchor pattern, and the result transports across defensible measurement routes and domains. Evidence against a conjunct includes valid redundancy across adequate diagnostic coverage, a negative risk difference favoring the ablated model, or repeated selective ablations that leave independent anchors unchanged.

An empty diagnostic set is not evidence for necessity. It is underidentification. A globally disruptive manipulation is not a clean falsifier. It is a coupled or unlicensed test. A failed operationalization may motivate a better estimator, but repeated post hoc replacement of estimators after adverse outcomes undermines the research program. Prospective revision criteria are therefore as important as favorable results.

## Chapter summary

A conjunctive theory cannot establish the necessity of its parts from diagonal all-pass and all-fail data. The full and leave-one-out rules disagree only when the omitted component fails and every retained component passes. Those diagnostic cases must have adequate coverage and independent held-out anchors. Criterion, mechanism, and measurement ablations ask different questions; causal claims require selective change and preserved referent identity. Profile rules must also be propagated through the candidate lattice before making bearer-level claims. Five audit outcomes preserve the difference between support, redundancy, coupling, underidentification, and invalid inference. Grouped ablations test stronger forms of minimality but still do not establish sufficiency against every rival.

## Key terms

- **Anti-circularity firewall:** prospective separation of hypotheses, component measures, anchors, data splits, and adjudication rules.
- **Bearer-output disagreement coverage:** target-distribution weight on which rival rules produce different licensed minimal-bearer sets.
- **Causal necessity:** anchor change following a selective change to a component’s physical realization.
- **Criterion ablation:** removal of a term from a decision rule.
- **Diagnostic coverage $\Gamma_k$:** target-distribution weight in the profile region where full and ablated rules disagree.
- **Diagnostic set $\mathcal D_k$:** cases in which component $k$ fails while every retained component passes.
- **Incremental empirical necessity:** held-out anchor advantage of the full model over a declared ablated rival in diagnostic cases.
- **Measurement ablation:** removal of an estimator, sensor, feature family, or pipeline.
- **Strong empirical minimality:** superiority of the full model over every preregistered proper submodel under matched conditions.

# Chapter 9 - From Phenomenal Presence to Phenomenal Character

**Opening puzzle.** Suppose two visual states both satisfy every licensed Cø / N* presence condition. One is an experience of red; the other is an experience of green. The presence model says that both episodes are there for the candidate system. It does not yet explain their difference. Now suppose a machine-learning model produces a beautiful two-dimensional plot in which red and green stimuli form separate clusters. Has the plot explained why the experiences differ?

Two mistakes tempt us. The first is to ask a presence theory to do work it was not designed to do. The second is to rename any structured neural or computational embedding a “quality space” and treat its geometry as phenomenal. The character extension avoids both by using a two-layer architecture. A licensed positive presence gate is established independently. Then a content-specific, perturbation-derived causal geometry is mapped to independently anchored phenomenal relations through a bridge that must predict held-out contents and intervention-driven changes.

**Why it matters.** Presence asks whether there is something it is like. Character asks what it is like. These questions interact, but their evidence and failure modes must not be merged.

## Chapter map

This chapter will help you:

- preserve the two-layer separation between phenomenal presence and phenomenal character;
- distinguish causal-trajectory geometry from a decoder embedding or activation snapshot;
- build phenomenal anchors independently of the proposed causal geometry;
- test a bridge through held-out prediction, nuisance comparison, invariance, and deformation;
- interpret character up to the symmetries the evidence leaves unbroken;
- represent modality, relational location, intensity, valence, and temporal path without forcing one Euclidean space; and
- explain why bridge failure need not reverse a presence result.

The guiding question is not “Can we draw the states?” but “Can a frozen causal structure predict independent phenomenal relations, including how those relations change under intervention?”

## 1. Presence is a gate, not another quality coordinate

Let $P(S,c;\Theta_P)$ be the preregistered empirical presence decision for system $S$, content $c$, and frozen presence specification $\Theta_P$. The value $P=1$ represents a licensed positive result. Negative, indeterminate, or unlicensed results do not enter an ordinary phenomenal-character attribution in this framework. For a nonpositive presence result, write $\chi(S,c)=\bot_P$. The symbol means “character attribution not licensed by the presence gate”; it does not mean a zero vector, blank quale, or measured absence of quality.

In the bearer-complete version of Cø / N*, a positive implementation requires an evidentially resolved boundary and membership in the estimated inclusion-minimal positive-bearer set. This is an evidence-relative decision. Researchers do not directly observe ontic membership - the mind-independent bearer relation posited by the theory; they estimate it under declared rules.

The second layer can be written:

$$
\text{phenomenal character}=Q_\Phi(G_c,\Delta).
$$

$G_c$ is the content-specific causal-trajectory geometry, $\Delta$ is the evaluation interval, and $\Phi$ is the complete bridge specification: boundary, grain, perturbation family, features, anchor construction, model family, nuisance controls, data splits, invariances, and thresholds. $Q_\Phi$ is the character readout licensed by that bridge.

Two non-implications protect the architecture:

$$
P(c_i)=P(c_j)=1\not\Rightarrow\chi(c_i)=\chi(c_j),
$$

and

$$
G_{c_i}\cong G_{c_j}\not\Rightarrow P(c_i)=P(c_j)=1.
$$

The first says that equally present states can differ in character. The second says that similar or isomorphic representational geometries do not manufacture presence. A nonconscious process can have rich structure.

**Plain language.** Passing the presence gate says an episode is admitted to the character question. It does not place the episode at “more red” or “less painful,” and an attractive map cannot open the gate from the other side.

**Analogy: admission to a museum and location within it.** A ticket determines whether a visitor may enter the museum. Once inside, a floor plan locates the visitor among galleries. Admission is not one coordinate on the floor plan, and drawing someone at the sculpture gallery does not prove they entered through a valid gate.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Museum admission is an institutional rule, while the presence gate is an empirical and constitutive hypothesis. A visitor exists independently of the ticket, whereas the theory concerns whether a physical organization is phenomenally present at all. The analogy only separates gating from differentiation.

**Source note.** *From Phenomenal Presence to Phenomenal Character* makes the two non-implications explicit and treats bridge outcomes as modular with respect to the presence result.

## 2. What a causal-trajectory geometry contains

$G_c$ is not a single activation vector, an unconstrained latent space, or any embedding that separates stimulus labels. It is the neighborhood of content $c$ within a structure derived from causal trajectories under a frozen family of admissible perturbations. Depending on the domain, it may include:

- distances among distributions of content-specific responses;
- directed transitions and their asymmetries;
- timing, direction, recurrence, and occupancy;
- route sensitivity under intervention; and
- local topology or stratification where one global metric fails.

The structure need not be Euclidean. A metric requires nonnegative, symmetric distances, identity of indiscernibles, and the triangle inequality. Real phenomenal or causal relations may be asymmetric, partially undefined, context-sensitive, or organized into modality-specific strata. A directed graph, pseudometric, ordinal structure, or stratified manifold may be more faithful than points on a flat plane.

The causal neighborhood must also be bounded. If every remote possible state were treated as constitutive of the current quality, an intervention on a never-visited distant region could change the predicted experience without changing any locally instantiated relation. The bridge specification should therefore freeze a neighborhood radius, occupancy rule, and time scale. A whole-structure version and a locally instantiated-relations version can then be compared by selectively changing remote geometry while preserving the local neighborhood.

**Checkpoint.** Ask of any proposed “quality geometry”: What interventions generated its relations? Which contents and transitions are included? Which metric assumptions were tested rather than plotted by default?

**Analogy: a city map versus a photograph of traffic.** A photograph records where cars happen to be at one moment. A useful transport map records routes, travel times, one-way streets, bottlenecks, and how closures change possible journeys. An activation snapshot resembles the photograph; a causal-trajectory geometry aims to resemble the route structure.

**Where the analogy breaks.** City routes are designed and externally surveyed, while neural or artificial causal relations must be estimated under intervention. Phenomenal relations may also involve valence, intensity, and meaning that are not reducible to travel distance. The analogy highlights counterfactual structure, not a literal spatial model of experience.

Decoding accuracy is therefore weaker than bridge fit. A decoder can exploit stimulus artifacts, response preparation, task difficulty, or a feature that separates labels without tracking experienced similarity. A causal geometry must survive controls for those nuisance structures and predict phenomenal relations that were not used to construct it.

## 3. Build the phenomenal target independently

The target quality structure, call it $\mathcal Q$, should be estimated in anchor cases using multiple converging methods. Possible sources include similarity judgments, discrimination thresholds, confusion matrices, generalization, adaptation, cross-context matching, and judgments of transitions. Reports can contribute in calibrated report-capable participants without becoming the minimal presence criterion. The separation is between evidence streams and inferential roles, not an absolute ban on all reports.

No single measure defines $\mathcal Q$. Similarity ratings may reflect task strategy. Discrimination thresholds can be asymmetric or context-sensitive. Confusion matrices mix perceptual and decisional processes. Adaptation may alter early encoding or attention. Convergence across partially independent routes is stronger; conflict beyond a preregistered tolerance produces an indeterminate target geometry.

The feature operator $\psi_\Phi(G_c,\Delta)$ exposes exactly which distances, transitions, topological properties, occupancy patterns, and temporal paths the bridge may use. Its dimensionality and preprocessing are frozen inside $\Phi$. A bridge $F_\Phi$ maps those causal features to the independently estimated quality structure:

$$
G_c\xrightarrow{\psi_\Phi}\text{frozen causal features}
\xrightarrow{F_\Phi}\text{predicted phenomenal relations}.
$$

Training, validation, and testing must remain separate. A high in-sample correlation can always reward flexibility. The stronger test is whether a bridge trained on some contents predicts the neighborhood of a held-out content and the direction and magnitude of phenomenal change after a geometry-changing intervention.

**Method note.** Draw two data-lineage streams. One runs from interventions to causal trajectories to $G_c$. The other runs from independently validated anchor procedures to $\mathcal Q$. Mark the first point where they meet. If they meet before the frozen bridge, inspect for leakage.

### Thought experiment: The perfect color decoder

A classifier identifies red, orange, green, and blue stimuli with 99.9% accuracy from neural data. Its embedding places the four classes in a neat circle. The classifier was trained on response labels collected during the same task. Independent similarity judgments place orange near red, but the decoder places orange equally far from every other color. A selective adaptation changes experienced similarity between orange and red while leaving decoder accuracy unchanged.

**Stage 1.** List the accomplishments the decoder has genuinely achieved.

**Stage 2.** Identify which facts prevent its embedding from functioning as an independently validated character bridge.

**Stage 3.** Propose a causal-trajectory geometry and a held-out test that could do more.

**Stage 4.** State what would follow if the decoder remained excellent while the bridge failed.

The exercise trains scope discipline. Label prediction may be scientifically useful even when it does not explain phenomenal relations.

## 4. The deformation test is the causal center

A supported bridge should do more than fit a static relation. A preregistered intervention changes $G_c$ in a specified way. The frozen bridge predicts which phenomenal distances should contract or expand, which transition order should change, or which path feature should be altered. The phenomenal deformation is then measured independently.

For example, fit a bridge using red, orange, and green while holding blue out. Baseline success requires predicting blue’s neighborhood from causal relations without training on blue’s phenomenal anchors. A stronger test changes one causal distance through adaptation or perturbation and predicts the corresponding change in an independently measured phenomenal relation. Stimulus distance, semantics, arousal, attention, motor preparation, and task demand should be compared as nuisance models.

A bridge is supported only relative to explicit rules. It should:

1. predict held-out phenomenal distances or neighborhoods better than declared nulls;
2. preserve validated transition structure and asymmetries;
3. survive coordinate rotations, harmless sensor relabeling, rescaling, and reasonable preprocessing;
4. outperform stimulus, semantic, arousal, and response-preparation nuisances;
5. predict intervention-driven deformation; and
6. transport only under a separate bridge license.

Baseline fit and intervention fit should be reported separately. Otherwise, a flexible model can hide a failed causal prediction behind an impressive static correlation.

**Do not infer.** If a selective change in $G_c$ produces no predicted phenomenal change under valid anchors, the tested bridge is threatened. The positive presence result need not change. Modularity is not optional bookkeeping; it identifies which claim failed.

### Thought experiment: The geometry transplant

Two systems have matched local causal geometries for four contents. In System H, the bridge was calibrated using multiple human phenomenal anchors. In System A, the same geometry appears in a materially different artificial substrate. No phenomenal anchors are available from A, but A has a frozen, licensed positive presence result, including a resolved minimal-bearer output.

**Stage 1.** Separate the presence claim, within-H character claim, and cross-system character claim.

**Stage 2.** Specify what a bridge-transport license would require beyond geometric isomorphism.

**Stage 3.** Consider a remote-region intervention that changes the whole geometry while preserving each content’s occupied local neighborhood. What would whole-space and local-relation bridges predict?

**Stage 4.** State the strongest conditional character attribution available if transport remains unresolved.

This thought experiment exposes a common shortcut: role-preserving transport of the presence profile does not automatically transport every calibrated character relation.

## 5. Symmetry limits absolute labels

Suppose a transformation preserves every measured distance, transition, valence relation, memory relation, and causal role in $\mathcal Q$. The evidence cannot distinguish a point $q$ from its transformed counterpart. The warranted attribution is therefore an equivalence class:

$$
Q_\Phi(G_c,\Delta)=
\left[F_\Phi(\psi_\Phi(G_c,\Delta))\right]_{\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal Q)}.
$$

$\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal Q)$ is the set of automorphisms: transformations that preserve all warranted relations in the quality structure. The brackets mean that the character is identified only up to those surviving symmetries.

An unlabeled equilateral triangle makes the point vivid. Any permutation of its three vertices preserves all pairwise distances. Purely relational evidence cannot say which vertex has an absolute name. Add a validated asymmetry - perhaps one vertex has a distinctive negative valence or directed transition - and fewer permutations remain.

This is a disciplined response to inverted-spectrum concerns. A global red-green swap that preserves every measured relation remains underdetermined. The theory should not assign an absolute label by assertion. Additional asymmetrical anchors can shrink the equivalence class, but only if they are independently warranted.

**Analogy: rotating a constellation chart.** A chart can be rotated while preserving distances and shapes among stars. If no “up” direction is independently marked, the orientation is underdetermined. A coastline or compass direction breaks the symmetry. Likewise, relational character evidence fixes only what its validated asymmetries can distinguish.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Stars have observer-independent spatial coordinates within physical frames, while the character problem concerns the relation between causal and phenomenal structures. The analogy illustrates symmetry and identifiability, not the ontology of qualia.

**Plain language.** The theory should claim no more individuality than its relations can identify. Underdetermination is a result, not a blank check for arbitrary labels.

## 6. Character may require more than one geometry

A structured character profile can be written:

$$
\chi_c=(m_c,[q_c],I_c,A_c,\Pi_c).
$$

$m_c$ is a modality stratum; $[q_c]$ is a symmetry-qualified relational location; $I_c$ is intensity; $A_c$ is a valence field or vector; and $\Pi_c$ is the temporal path profile. The tuple is not assumed to be exhaustive.

Color examples are useful but can distort the research agenda. Audition involves pitch, timbre, loudness, rhythm, and directed temporal order. Olfaction includes mixture effects and context dependence that may violate simple Euclidean relations. Pain separates intensity, unpleasantness, location, urgency, controllability, and temporal pattern. Interoceptive states may combine bodily location with action tendency and value. Cross-modal relations should be posited only where independent anchors justify them.

For pain, a one-dimensional scale can hide important differences. Two episodes may have equal reported intensity but differ in unpleasantness or urgency. $N_3$ asks whether the pain content is recurrently present. $\Pi_c$ distinguishes steady, pulsing, spreading, or anticipatory temporal paths. A credible bridge may need directed graphs, stratified spaces, and value fields rather than a universal metric.

**Checkpoint.** Before forcing data into a common Euclidean space, ask which relations are undefined, asymmetric, context-sensitive, or modality-specific. A graph with honest gaps can be more informative than a smooth but invented map.

## 7. Four bridge outcomes and a modular error matrix

The bridge can be **supported**, **refuted**, **indeterminate**, or **unlicensed**. Combined with the presence result, these statuses prevent cross-layer overreach.

If presence is positive and the bridge is supported, the tested relational character claim is supported. If presence is positive and the bridge is refuted, the episode remains present under the model, but this mapping fails. If the bridge is indeterminate, the character relation is unresolved. If the bridge is unlicensed, its procedure cannot be interpreted.

If the presence model makes a licensed negative prediction, this framework licenses neither ordinary character attribution nor a specific claim about which character is absent; measured representational geometry nevertheless remains scientifically meaningful. If presence is indeterminate, representational and bridge evidence may be studied conditionally without converting it into an unconditional phenomenal claim. If presence is unlicensed, repair the gate before combined inference.

**Do not infer.** A failed bridge does not show that no phenomenal character exists. It shows that the proposed mapping did not earn the tested relation. A beautiful bridge cannot rescue an invalid presence assessment.

Specification stability matters. Let $\mathcal N(\Phi_0)$ be a frozen family varying boundary, grain, causal metric, anchor combination, preprocessing, and bridge family. The weights assigned to its alternative specifications must also be fixed before bridge outcomes are inspected. A robustness summary can report how often the bridge status matches the primary specification across those weighted alternatives. This is a stability frequency, not a posterior probability that the character identity is true. Report which choices reverse the outcome.

## 8. Rival lenses, evidence, and explanatory limits

Quality-space theories emphasize the relational organization of perceptual qualities. Representational-geometry and neural-manifold methods provide tools for estimating relations among population states. Cø / N* borrows the pressure toward relational structure but adds a presence gate, perturbation-derived causal geometry, independent phenomenal anchors, and a frozen bridge. It rejects the inference from “structured representation” to “phenomenal structure” without validation.

Integrated Information Theory also treats intrinsic causal structure as important, but the present character program does not simply identify an IIT structure with experience. Exact comparison requires a dated IIT version and matched objects of analysis. Workspace and higher-order theories may explain how character information becomes globally usable, reportable, or represented at a higher order; the present bridge targets phenomenal relations after minimal presence is licensed. Representationalism and structuralism offer philosophical interpretations of why a successful relation might be constitutive, predictive, or explanatory.

A successful bridge would not by itself solve the “hard problem” or prove that every intrinsic phenomenal fact is captured. It could nevertheless achieve substantial scientific explanation: held-out relational prediction, causal deformation, invariance, fault localization, and transport under explicit licenses. The difference between predictive success and constitutive identity should remain visible.

Evidence directly challenges the bridge if selective changes in $G_c$ fail to produce predicted phenomenal changes, phenomenal geometry changes while $G_c$ remains stable across reasonable specifications, held-out performance falls to nuisance parity, a simpler stimulus model wins after causal controls, claimed transport fails, or small reasonable specification changes reverse status. The response should be revision or rejection of $Q_\Phi$, not post hoc redefinition of the geometry or anchors.

**Source note.** The character paper presents causal deformation and symmetry-qualified attribution as central safeguards against merely renaming an embedding. It explicitly leaves open whether valence, meaning, or bodily urgency require richer structures.

## Chapter summary

Phenomenal presence and phenomenal character require two linked but modular layers. A positive presence gate licenses the character question; structured geometry alone does not open the gate. $G_c$ must be a content-specific causal-trajectory structure rather than an attractive activation embedding. # Chapter 10 - Reading Consciousness from the Schematic

**Opening puzzle.** A trained engineer looks at an electrical schematic and says, “With the source connected, this switch closed, and these component values, the motor will turn.” A novice sees ink, lines, and unfamiliar symbols. The motor’s motion is not pictured inside the ink, yet the engineer can derive it from learned component meanings, operating conditions, and counterfactual dependencies. Suppose Cø / N* survives demanding empirical tests. Could a trained researcher inspect a role-annotated causal schematic and read phenomenal presence from it in a comparably structured way?

The question goes beyond prediction. A marker might correlate reliably with consciousness evidence. A black-box law might predict presence across held-out cases. A **constitutive schematic** would add an interpretable route: the reader reconstructs a bearer, realized operation, unity, system-level causal presence, and temporal presence, then applies the explicit identity hypothesis that this role-complete organization is minimal phenomenal presence.

**Do not infer.** A readable diagram does not experience anything, and readability does not prove the identity. The actual system must realize the causal organization. Independent evidence must still bear on whether the identity is true.

## Chapter map

This chapter will enable you to:

- distinguish a representation, a causal realization, and a possible bearer;
- reconstruct the Cø / N* role vector from component results;

## 1. Three objects that must never be collapsed

The first object is the **representation**: a printed diagram, equation, graph, or digital schematic. It carries symbols and arrows whose meanings are learned. The second is the **realization**: the running physical system with effective causal dynamics at a specified grain, regime, and interval. The third is the possible **bearer**: a boundary-qualified, causally admissible candidate that may realize a unified episode.

Confusing these objects produces several errors. A diagram that depicts recurrent loops is not itself recurrently maintaining a content in the theoretical sense. Software that simulates a network is not automatically a causal realization at the modeled grain. A large system that contains all relevant components is not automatically the minimal bearer if a proper subsystem also passes. Conversely, a physical realization need not resemble the visual layout of its schematic.

**Plain language.** The map, the running machine, and the thing whose state the map describes are related, but they are not interchangeable.

**Analogy: a musical score, performance, and ensemble.** A score represents timed relations among parts. A performance realizes those relations in sound. The ensemble is the organized group whose performance is being described. A score can be copied without producing music; a performance can use different instruments while preserving key relations; and it can be unclear whether a guest player belongs to the ensemble or functions as external support.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Musical membership is partly conventional, and performances are interpreted through human practices. Candidate-bearer individuation is supposed to rest on causal autonomy, mapping, minimality, and separability. The analogy separates representation, realization, and bearer, but it does not validate phenomenal attribution.

Let the role-annotated schematic be $\mathfrak S_{N^*}$. The proposed readout has two arrows:

$$
\mathfrak S_{N^*}
\Longrightarrow
\boldsymbol\rho_{C_0}
\Longrightarrow
C_0.
$$

The first arrow is mechanistic interpretation: use public component meanings and results to reconstruct a role profile. The second arrow applies the exposed constitutive identity hypothesis. That second arrow must remain visible. Hiding it inside a label such as “consciousness score” turns a substantive claim into an apparent measurement fact.

**Source note.** *Reading Consciousness from the Schematic* frames the schematic as a representation from which a trained reader reconstructs an intermediate role profile. It repeatedly distinguishes this explanatory achievement from validation of the identity.

## 2. The role vector makes the theory inspectable

The intermediate profile is:

$$
\boldsymbol\rho_{C_0}=
\langle
\text{bearer},
\text{realized operation},
\text{unity},
\text{system-level presence},
\text{temporal presence}
\rangle.
$$

Each entry has a distinct source.

- A stable member of the causally admissible candidate family $\mathcal P_\Theta$ supplies a licensed referent for profile testing. Resolved inclusion-minimal positive-bearer membership supplies the theory-relative subject bearer.
- $V$ supplies realized operation in the declared regime. An inert diagram, failed preparation, or out-of-domain state does not qualify.
- $N_1$ supplies unity: the content is irreducibly joint rather than a pile of independent events.
- $N_2$ supplies system-level causal presence: the content can modulate functionally diverse recipients without report being required.
- $N_3$ supplies bounded temporal presence: the content is recurrently or metastably maintained rather than an evanescent feedforward transition or indefinite fixation.

These meanings should not be defined by repeating “consciousness.” Integration is operationalized through excess joint causal contribution, availability through content-specific counterfactual reach, and recurrence through return-dependent temporal stability. The constitutive claim then says that a viable, bearer-complete organization with these roles is minimal phenomenal presence.

**Checkpoint.** Can you describe each role and its characteristic failure without using the words *conscious*, *experience*, or *phenomenal*? If not, decomposable semantics may be circular or too vague.

This role vector also makes limitations visible. It addresses minimal presence, not necessarily attention, introspection, autobiographical selfhood, report, intelligence, or full character. The character extension adds a second readout after the gate. A change in $G_c$ with the presence roles intact may change predicted character rather than presence.

**Analogy: a medical pathway rather than a single lab value.** A clinician may interpret several independently meaningful findings - circulation, oxygenation, organ function, and causal response - to reconstruct a physiological state. The interpretation is more informative than a one-number score because different failures suggest different mechanisms and responses.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Medical states often have mature reference standards, long validation histories, and intervention outcomes that consciousness science lacks. The analogy illustrates compositional and fault-localizing explanation, not equal evidential maturity.

## 3. Marker, black-box law, or constitutive schematic?

Three explanatory levels should be distinguished.

A **correlational marker** covaries with independent consciousness evidence. It can be useful for detection while its relation to the target remains incidental, indirect, or confounded.

A **black-box law** says that licensed positive-bearer membership built from the full profile reliably guarantees $C_0$ in the declared domain. This is a sufficiency claim; the biconditional’s necessity direction requires separate evidence from well-supported $C_0$ cases outside the positive-bearer set. Yet the transition from organization to phenomenal presence may remain opaque.

A **constitutive schematic** adds stable role semantics, compositional reconstruction, counterfactual fault structure, and the identity claim that the role-complete organization is the physical form of minimal phenomenal presence. It aims to make the proposal understandable even to someone who rejects it.

The difference between the last two can be written:

$$
\text{black-box sufficiency}:X\Rightarrow C_0,
$$

$$
\text{structurally legible sufficiency}:X\Rightarrow\boldsymbol\rho\Rightarrow C_0.
$$

The intermediate $\boldsymbol\rho$ is not decorative prose. Its parts should support distinct intervention predictions, partial failures, and transport tests.

**Why it matters.** Accurate prediction and explanation are related achievements, not synonyms. Adding a readable mechanism does not erase the need for accuracy; achieving accuracy does not guarantee a readable mechanism.

### Thought experiment: Two high-performing classifiers

Classifier A receives a thousand neural features and returns “present” or “absent” with excellent held-out performance. Its internal representation is not stable across retraining, and no one can state which failure should follow from changing any feature family. Classifier B has slightly lower held-out accuracy but reconstructs the five-role profile, abstains when the bearer is unresolved, and predicts different anchor changes under selective $N_1$, $N_2$, and $N_3$ faults.

**Stage 1.** Classify each system as marker, black-box law, constitutive schematic, or some intermediate achievement.

**Stage 2.** Identify what evidence would be required before calling either classifier a validated consciousness test.

**Stage 3.** Design a prospective fault test on which their predictions differ.

**Stage 4.** Discuss whether B’s interpretability could compensate for lower accuracy, and specify the decision context in which your answer changes.

The exercise resists a false choice. A mature program should seek calibration and structural legibility while reporting tradeoffs rather than awarding automatic priority to either.

## 4. Four conditions for schematic legibility

Let $M$ be a structured model, $o$ the target state, and $K$ the publicly teachable component and representational knowledge.

**Notation note.** Symbols are locally scoped. Here $D,C,F,E$ name the four legibility conditions; they are not the scientific-status $D$, target $C$, or evidence $E$ used in Chapter 7.

Define:

$$
\operatorname{Legible}(M,o\mid K)\Longleftrightarrow D\land C\land F\land E.
$$

The four conditions are:

1. **Decomposable semantics ($D$).** Elements have stable role meanings that do not merely repeat the target outcome.
2. **Compositional readout ($C$).** A trained reader can derive an intermediate organization profile and apply an explicit rule.
3. **Counterfactual fault localization ($F$).** Licensed changes support differentiated predictions about which role fails or changes.
4. **Evidential independence ($E$).** The readout procedure is not counted as evidence that its own constitutive rule is true.

The conditions form a conjunctive minimum for strong legibility, but each can be studied in a graded way. Readers may agree on component meanings yet fail to combine them. They may combine them but make only generic “system failed” predictions. They may localize faults reproducibly while validation labels were circularly generated. Reporting the pattern is more informative than a single interpretability score.

**Method note.** Audit $D$ and $C$ at the level of the common law sheet or representational system. Audit $F$ for each licensed profile or contrast. Audit $E$ for each validation stream. A boundary failure in one case does not erase stable semantics for every case, while circular labels in one study can invalidate its confirmation claim without making the schematic unreadable.

**Analogy: learning to read a circuit.** The learner first acquires symbol meanings, then learns how component laws compose, then predicts what happens when a switch opens or a resistor fails, and finally compares those predictions with measurements not created by the diagram itself. This sequence parallels $D$, $C$, $F$, and $E$.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Electrical theory has mature laws, reliable instruments, and direct operating-state checks. Consciousness science has contested targets, imperfect anchors, boundary uncertainty, and substantial theory disagreement. The circuit analogy proposes a target epistemic form, not current parity.

Legibility can fail while prediction remains useful. If a one-number score predicts well but its elements have no stable meanings, $D$ fails. If roles are meaningful but no rule combines them, $C$ fails. If all interventions collapse the system globally, $F$ remains unresolved. If outcome labels are derived from $N^*$ itself, $E$ fails. None of these failures proves that the system is nonconscious; they identify limits in the explanatory and evidential architecture.

## 5. Faults operate at profile and bearer levels

The schematic’s fault map is one of its main explanatory claims. Different failures should have different interpretations.

If the frozen $\mathcal P_\Theta$ referent loses autonomy, causal admissibility, or cross-condition mapping, component inference becomes unlicensed because the entity under comparison is unstable. A viability failure means the operating regime no longer supports the relevant dynamics. On a stable referent, a selective $N_1$ failure is a profile-level loss of unity. A selective $N_2$ failure is profile-level causal isolation. A selective $N_3$ failure is profile-level temporal evanescence. A change in $G_c$ under an intact presence gate changes the predicted character relation.

After a component fault, recompute the all-pass set across the frozen candidate lattice. The final bearer-level result may be:

- loss of the former bearer;
- emergence of a smaller or alternative bearer;
- several separable minimal bearers;
- unresolved multiplicity; or
- a licensed negative prediction if a decisive failure or minimality result applies on a resolved domain.

**Do not infer.** A profile fault in the original candidate does not by itself prove that consciousness vanished everywhere in the larger system. Nor does the persistence of some all-pass subsystem prove that the original subject remained unchanged.

### Thought experiment: The modular ship

A research vessel contains two tightly coupled control modules. Under baseline conditions, the combined candidate is stable and inclusion-minimal. An intervention selectively blocks content-specific routing between modules while leaving each module viable and internally recurrent. Afterward, one module passes all profile conditions for content $c$, the other passes for content $d$, and separability evidence is incomplete.

**Stage 1.** State the profile-level fault in the original combined candidate.

**Stage 2.** Reconstruct the possible bearer-level outputs without choosing one by intuition.

**Stage 3.** Identify the mapping and separability evidence needed to distinguish relocation, plurality, and unresolved multiplicity.

**Stage 4.** Explain which parts of a schematic readout remain legible even if the final bearer attribution is indeterminate.

The case shows why schematic competence includes correct abstention. A reader who announces one subject merely because a diagram is detailed has not demonstrated legibility; they have ignored the boundary rule.

## 6. Teachability is testable, but agreement is not confirmation

A public schematic should be learnable by independent investigators. A three-stage protocol can test this.

First, train readers on component semantics, candidate and bearer distinctions, worked profiles, and correct abstention. Second, test blind readout on unseen cases: readers reconstruct roles, classify outcomes, localize faults, and mark the step that depends on the constitutive identity. Third, test transfer to a materially different realization whose role organization is claimed to be preserved.

Measure inter-reader agreement, calibration, fault specificity, abstention quality, and transport accuracy. A reader who refuses to classify an unstable-boundary case may be more competent than one who always issues a verdict. Disagreements should be coded by source: semantics, combination rule, boundary mapping, uncertainty policy, fault attribution, or identity stance.

High agreement establishes that the readout is teachable under $K$. It does not establish that $C_0$ is identical to the role profile. Two readers can reconstruct the same organization and rationally disagree about the constitutive claim. This is a virtue: the theory’s empirical and philosophical commitments remain exposed.

**Checkpoint.** Every teachability report should contain the sentence: “Agreement on the readout is evidence for public interpretability; it is not independent evidence that the identity is true.”

Five empirical payoffs should be tracked separately:

- **Role-preserving transport:** does the same role profile survive a materially different realization?
- **Prospective fault prediction:** do component-specific changes forecast differentiated failures?
- **Estimator convergence:** do independent measures recover the same intermediate roles?
- **Compression:** does the role profile omit causal detail while preserving rival-distinguishing predictions?
- **Teachability:** can independent readers reconstruct, abstain, and localize faults on held-out cases?

Success on one payoff does not validate all five. A schematic can be teachable but fail transport, compressive but poorly calibrated, or fault-localizing while the identity remains empirically unsupported.

## 7. Objections clarify what the claim does and does not say

### Circularity

An objector may argue that the roles appear consciousness-like only because the theory describes them using words such as *unity* and *presence*. The reply must be operational, not rhetorical. Each role should be stated through consciousness-independent causal tests. If the role meanings cannot be stated without assuming the conclusion, $D$ fails. If the roles are readable but readout-generated labels are reused to validate the identity, $E$ fails. Passing both conditions leaves the identity substantive and contestable rather than definitional.

### The zombie challenge

A physical duplicate with the complete organization but no experience would defeat sufficiency if independently established. Merely describing such a duplicate as conceivable does not provide an empirical case, but it does identify the deepest constitutive disagreement. The schematic thesis does not dissolve that disagreement by making the roles vivid. It exposes exactly where the opponent denies the second arrow.

### External observation

A researcher can observe that a system realizes the proposed organization without undergoing its token experience. Under a separately supported identity hypothesis, observing that organization licenses attribution of $C_0$; this remains distinct from inferring token character or occupying the bearer’s first-person position. Scientific observation need not reproduce its object inside the observer. Whether organization is sufficient for warranted attribution remains the empirical and constitutive question.

### Functionalism and implementation

The proposal is a physicalist information-dynamical functionalism, not unrestricted input-output equivalence. Static lookup tables, behaviorally equivalent scripts, and merely isomorphic diagrams do not automatically qualify. Effective causal realization, correct grain, stable boundary, timing, and viability do real work. A materially different implementation may qualify only if the public role profile and its counterfactual organization transport.

**Why it matters.** The strongest objections do not show that the schematic is meaningless. They locate disagreements about operational independence, causal realization, and constitutive identity. A theory becomes easier to test when opponents can say exactly which arrow they reject.

**Source note.** The schematic paper treats the zombie and external-observation objections as challenges to the constitutive identity, not as reasons to abandon public causal description. Its conclusion remains explicitly conditional.

## 8. Evidence, limits, and rival explanatory ideals

Mechanistic explanation emphasizes organized entities, activities, dependencies, and counterfactuals. Cø / N* seeks this structure through its role vector and fault map. Black-box predictive approaches may outperform it in some settings, especially where mechanisms are poorly understood. Correlational biomarkers can be clinically useful without constitutive interpretation. The levels should be compared on their actual aims rather than ranked by label.

Evidence favors schematic legibility when independent readers learn stable meanings, reconstruct roles on held-out cases, abstain appropriately, predict distinct faults prospectively, converge across estimators, and transfer to new implementations under preserved causal roles. Evidence against legibility includes persistent semantic disagreement, absence of a compositional rule, generic rather than differentiated fault predictions, circular validation, failure of transport, or compression that discards rival-distinguishing information.

Evidence for legibility is not yet evidence for the identity. The constitutive claim additionally faces the full empirical program: sufficiency challenges from licensed all-pass cases with strong independent evidence against presence, necessity challenges from well-supported conscious cases outside the positive-bearer set, component audits, rival comparisons, and transport tests. Structural intelligibility can coexist with empirical failure.

The analogy to electrical schematics has a strict limit. Mature electrical models are embedded in well-confirmed theory and measurement practices. Cø / N* is a new, adversarial research program. The analogy sets a standard - public semantics, composition, counterfactuals, fault localization, and independent validation - rather than awarding the model the authority of engineering.

## Chapter summary

Schematic legibility separates the representation, running causal realization, and possible bearer. A trained reader reconstructs a five-part role profile - bearer, realized operation, unity, system-level causal presence, and temporal presence - before applying the exposed constitutive hypothesis. This differs from both a correlational marker and an opaque predictive law. Strong legibility requires decomposable semantics, compositional readout, differentiated fault localization, and evidential independence. Faults must be interpreted first at the profile level and then propagated through the candidate lattice. Teachability, agreement, transport, compression, and fault prediction are empirical achievements, but none alone proves that the role-complete organization is phenomenal presence. The electrical analogy names a scientific ideal, not an attained evidential parity.

## Key terms

- **Black-box law:** a reliable predictive relation whose transition from organization to target remains uninterpreted.
- **Compositional readout ($C$):** derivation of a whole role profile from stable component meanings and an explicit combination rule.
- **Constitutive schematic:** a role-complete, fault-localizing model paired with an explicit identity claim.
- **Counterfactual fault localization ($F$):** differentiated predictions about which role changes under licensed component changes.
- **Decomposable semantics ($D$):** stable role meanings that do not merely repeat the outcome.
- **Evidential independence ($E$):** validation that does not use the schematic’s own output as its truth label.
- **Realization:** the running physical system that instantiates effective causal dynamics.
- **Representation:** the diagram, equation, or model presented for interpretation.
- **Role vector $\boldsymbol\rho_{C_0}$:** bearer, operation, unity, system-level presence, and temporal presence.
- **Schematic legibility:** publicly teachable reconstruction, fault reasoning, and evidence separation under $D\land C\land F\land E$.
