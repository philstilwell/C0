# Chapter 11 — Biological and Clinical Applications

## Opening puzzle: the silent patient

A patient opens her eyes but does not follow commands. On Monday, an auditory imagery task produces no usable signal; the recording is noisy and no one has confirmed that she can hear the instructions. On Thursday, during a higher-arousal period, a motor-imagery pattern appears repeatedly even though she still makes no movement. On Sunday, the pattern disappears during a low-arousal recording. Has consciousness appeared, vanished, and returned? Was it present all week? Or has the study not yet earned any of those conclusions?

The puzzle is difficult because behavior, measurement quality, physiological state, and phenomenal presence can all vary—and they need not vary together. Cø / N* asks us to separate them. Here **Cø** means minimal phenomenal presence: there is something it is like for the candidate system during the specified interval. **N\*** is the conjunction $N_1\land N_2\land N_3$: integration or synergy, system-wide causal availability, and recurrent stability. **V** is a separate viability condition. The theory's core hypothesis is conditional and exposed to failure:

$$
C_0(S,c,\tau) \leftrightarrow V(S,\tau)\land N_1(S,c,\tau)\land N_2(S,c,\tau)\land N_3(S,c,\tau).
$$

This compact equation states the source profile conjecture and suppresses bearer selection. Empirically, $H_*(S,c)=V\land N^*$ may be estimated for a frozen referent $S\in\mathcal P_\Theta$, but final subject attribution additionally requires $\operatorname{Resolved}_B(c;\Theta)$ and $S\in\mathcal B_{\min,\Theta}^{+}(c)$. Hatted estimates are evidence outputs, not the underlying ontic properties.

The symbols force a useful discipline. The claim is always about a candidate system $S$, a content or target $c$, and an interval $\tau$. A positive result for one content during one minute is not automatically a verdict about every experience the person had that day.

**Plain language.** A person can be unable to show consciousness while still being conscious. A test can also fail to find consciousness because the test was not capable of finding it in that moment.

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

## 1. Begin with the question, not the instrument

Clinical urgency tempts us to begin with a device: an EEG score, an fMRI task, a bedside scale, or a complexity index. Cø / N* begins one step earlier. What exactly are we trying to determine?

The fixed inference order is:

1. define the target $C$;
2. declare the candidate system, grain, operating regime, and interval;
3. audit whether the boundary of that candidate is resolved;
4. assess viability $V$ independently;
5. estimate $N_1$, $N_2$, and $N_3$ for the same candidate and target;
6. apply conflict, uncertainty, and validity rules;
7. state the model-relative scientific result; and
8. make any clinical or ethical recommendation separately.

The four possible scientific outcomes are **positive**, **negative**, **indeterminate**, and **unlicensed**. Positive and negative are not simply high and low scores. A negative requires a validated rule-out region and a fair opportunity for detection. Indeterminate means that relevant, valid evidence exists but does not converge strongly enough. Unlicensed means that a prerequisite failed: perhaps the sensory route was blocked, the candidate boundary shifted, the instrument was not calibrated for this population, or artifact made the recording uninterpretable.

**Why it matters.** Without this four-way vocabulary, “the test did not find it” easily becomes “it was not there.” In vulnerable patients, that linguistic slide can have enormous consequences.

Think of the workflow as an airport departure board. A flight may be listed as departed, cancelled, delayed, or unable to report because the data feed is down. “Unable to report” is not a hidden form of “cancelled.” Likewise, an unlicensed test result is not a disguised negative.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Airlines can check a centralized operational record. Consciousness research has no universal gold-standard record, so the classification rules and anchor evidence remain theory-laden and fallible.

**Checkpoint.** For the opening patient, write one target that is narrower than “Is she conscious?” Then specify an interval over which that target could be assessed.

An assessment record can be written as

$$
\mathcal A=\langle S,C,T,E,M,\mathcal V\rangle,
$$

where $S$ is the candidate system, $C$ the exact target, $T$ the test, $E$ the evidence, $M$ the model, and $\mathcal V$ the validity envelope. If the result is indeterminate, record why:

$$
P_R(\mathcal A)=\langle e,s,m,b,t\rangle.
$$

The five entries represent evidence scarcity, surrogate discordance, model dependence, boundary or grain sensitivity, and transport uncertainty. The tuple is a cause profile, not another consciousness score. A component can be absent, mild, or dominant; the point is to direct the next study toward the actual obstacle.

## 2. Waking perception is a calibration case, not a definition

Healthy waking adults offer abundant converging evidence. A dominant visual percept, for example, normally occurs in a viable system with integrated processing, causal availability to multiple functions, and recurrent temporal organization. Reports, discrimination, memory, eye movements, physiology, and perturbational responses can be compared. This makes waking perception useful for developing and stress-testing measures.

But the easy case can distort the theory. If every measure is chosen because it correlates with button presses in alert adults, report-related motor planning and executive demand may be built into the operational definition. Such a measure will then misclassify conditions in which experience remains but reporting fails.

**Do not infer.** A useful waking calibration does not show that wakefulness, responsiveness, or accurate report constitutes phenomenal presence.

### Thought experiment: The perfect mute

Imagine a reversible intervention that blocks all voluntary muscles and speech while leaving perception, memory, attention, internal reasoning, and the N* organization intact.

**Stage 1.** Before any alternative communication channel is available, what would a behavior-only test conclude?

**Stage 2.** A brain–computer interface later reveals reliable answers to novel questions. Which earlier inference changes: the patient's state, the evidence, or both?

**Stage 3.** Suppose the interface itself requires substantial executive control. What narrower claim can it support, and what broader claims remain open?

The case highlights a central distinction: evidence for presence can depend on a reporting route even if presence itself does not.

## 3. Sleep and dreaming: internal availability

Sleep makes the index $\tau$ impossible to ignore. A night is not one homogeneous state. REM periods, non-REM stages, brief awakenings, local sleep-like events, and transitions may support different component profiles.

During REM dreaming, motor output is greatly reduced and external interaction is limited. Yet dream contents may affect attention, emotion, memory formation, imagery, and other internal recipient classes. On this model, $N_2$ does not require outward broadcast. It requires content-specific causal availability across functionally different recipients within the candidate system. The relevant recipients and timescales may differ from those of waking task performance.

Retrospective dream report is useful but displaced. It occurs after the target interval and depends on encoding, preservation, awakening, comprehension, and willingness to report. A negative report therefore has multiple possible explanations.

Deep non-REM sleep may include sparse, episodic, or content-specific presence; it may also include periods that genuinely lack it. The model does not settle that question by stipulation. Repeated sampling, well-timed awakening, physiological characterization, and perturbational evidence must do the work. Perturbational measures can reduce reliance on behavior, but their interpretation still requires population- and regime-specific validation (Casali et al., 2013).

**Method note.** Report the distribution of outcomes across sampled intervals. “Seven licensed negatives, two positives, four indeterminate windows, and three unlicensed recordings” contains more information than a single label for the whole night.

An analogy is a wildlife survey conducted in a large forest. One empty camera-trap image does not establish that no animal was present anywhere that night. Repeated, well-placed sensors improve coverage, and a failed camera tells us less than an empty image from a functioning one.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Animals leave publicly measurable traces and can sometimes be directly counted. Phenomenal presence is inferred through imperfect anchors, so even excellent sampling does not remove the theory-relative character of the conclusion.

### Thought experiment: The dream with no archive

Suppose a person has a rich dream, but a temporary memory-blocking process prevents any trace from surviving awakening.

**Stage 1.** Would the later absence of report count against the dream's occurrence, or only against its retrieval?

**Stage 2.** During sleep, a content-specific perturbation changes several internal processes in a coordinated way. What role could that evidence play in estimating $N_2$ and $N_3$?

**Stage 3.** The same perturbation also partially wakes the sleeper. How does that threaten the inference?

## 4. Anesthesia is not one mechanism or one moment

“Under anesthesia” can hide differences among drug, dose, timing, physiology, sensory input, and individual response. Some agents alter thalamocortical organization, some reduce recurrent connectivity, and some change neuromodulatory conditions. Induction, maintenance, emergence, and recovery are different regimes.

A licensed negative under Cø / N* requires more than low responsiveness. Investigators must show that the target candidate had a fair detection opportunity, that relevant measures were valid under the agent and regime, and that the N* bundle failed for the specified content. If the candidate boundary reorganizes, positive-bearer status must be recomputed; one cannot assume that the pre-anesthetic bearer persists unchanged.

Near misses are especially informative. Dreaming-like experiences during anesthesia, disconnected responsiveness, and uneven recovery can separate motor output from internal dynamics. Different agents can also reveal whether a measure tracks consciousness-relevant organization or merely a pharmacological signature.

**Plain language.** Global physiological disruption is easy to produce but hard to interpret. A selective failure teaches more because fewer explanations change at once.

**Do not infer.** A low value on a consciousness-associated measure is not a licensed absence verdict unless that measure has a validated negative region in the same kind of state.

## 5. Disorders of consciousness and the negative-license checklist

Locked-in syndrome is the clearest warning against equating motor silence with experiential absence. A person may have a stable, viable N* organization but no ordinary path to speech or movement. Cognitive motor dissociation presents a related challenge: some behaviorally unresponsive patients show task-specific neural evidence of command following.

Clinical labels such as **unresponsive wakefulness syndrome** and **minimally conscious state** summarize observed behavioral patterns; they are not direct readings of phenomenal presence. In either group, arousal, sensory access, motor capacity, and test quality can fluctuate. Assessment should therefore be repeated across suitable windows, tied to a declared content or capacity, and reported with its negative license. One failed command-following trial is weak evidence of absence, especially when hearing, comprehension, or signal quality has not been established. Repeated behavioral assessment and recent work on cognitive motor dissociation illustrate why single observations can underclassify patients (Wannez et al., 2017; Bodien et al., 2024).

Such evidence is important but narrower than it first appears. Successful command following supports hearing or other sensory access, comprehension, task maintenance, and a content-specific response. It does not automatically establish continuous awareness, rich experience, or the exact boundary of the subject. Conversely, failure on the task is interpretable only when those auxiliary capacities had a fair opportunity to operate.

Before treating non-detection as a negative result, ask:

1. Was the target explicit and appropriate?
2. Was the candidate system stable enough to assess?
3. Did the patient receive the stimulus through a working route?
4. Were arousal, attention, comprehension, memory, and task demands adequate?
5. Was the measure validated in this injury class and operating regime?
6. Did recording quality and artifact controls pass?
7. Did the result enter a preregistered rule-out region?
8. Were plausible alternative causes of non-detection controlled?

If a required item fails, the appropriate result may be unlicensed. If all pass but credible measures conflict, indeterminate may be correct. Repeated assessment matters because both the patient's state and the test's quality can fluctuate.

### Thought experiment: Three windows, one person

Consider a fictional patient, Mira.

**Window A.** Low arousal, heavy artifact, and unconfirmed hearing accompany no command response.

**Window B.** During high arousal, two valid surrogates disagree while the candidate boundary remains stable.

**Window C.** Repeated, content-specific imagery modulation appears with route controls and a resolved minimal bearer, but there is no motor response.

For each window, ask:

1. Which of the four outcomes is even available under the evidence rules?
2. What is the dominant uncertainty source? Record the cause profile $P_R(\mathcal A)=\langle e,s,m,b,t\rangle$ rather than naming only one vague doubt.
3. What single next measurement would most directly address that source?
4. What broader conclusion would exceed the target?

**Checkpoint.** Referring to the same patient across windows does not by itself establish persistence of the same minimal conscious bearer. Track the patient-level mapping separately from the time-indexed scientific classification and from any claim that one mapped minimal bearer persists.

## 6. Blindsight and binocular rivalry: performance is not presence

In blindsight, a person can make above-chance forced choices about stimuli in a region they report as unseen. The performance establishes information sensitivity; it does not by itself settle whether the blind-field content is phenomenally present. Cø / N* directs attention to the content-specific component profile. Unconscious information may guide behavior while failing one or more of integration, diverse causal availability, and recurrent stability.

Binocular rivalry offers a complementary design. Physical stimulation can remain stable while the dominant percept alternates. Eye movements and other involuntary markers may help time those changes without requiring a report on every trial. The model predicts that content-specific N* should track the dominant content, but the markers are anchors or timing aids, not constituents by definition. Physical-alternation controls and report/no-report contrasts help separate perception from introspection and motor preparation (Frässle et al., 2014; Tsuchiya et al., 2015).

**Why it matters.** These cases replace the weak contrast “can perform versus cannot perform” with the sharper question “which causal role profile supports this content?”

## 7. Split systems: count candidates before counting subjects

Split-brain cases invite a dramatic question: one subject or two? The boundary-first approach slows the question down. Investigators should evaluate left, right, and residual joint candidates under a declared task and temporal horizon.

A subsystem qualifies as a candidate only if it shows sufficient autonomy, role completeness, stability, and separability. Shared subcortical regulation or longer-timescale coordination may support a larger candidate even when task-specific processing is lateralized. Results may differ across a one-second perceptual horizon and a day-long regulatory horizon without making the method arbitrary. What matters is that the horizons and candidate-generation rules were fixed before the preferred metaphysical answer was known.

**Method note.** Do not compare pre- and post-disconnection component scores as properties of one unchanged bearer unless boundary identity has been established. The intervention may change what the candidate system is.

Think of a university that divides into two campuses. Each campus may handle admissions, teaching, payroll, and governance independently, or the two may still depend on one central administration. Counting buildings does not settle how many institutions exist; functional autonomy and role completeness matter.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Universities are legal and social entities whose boundaries can be stipulated. Candidate conscious systems are proposed causal units, so their individuation must be supported by intervention and dynamical evidence rather than paperwork.

## 8. Evidence and limits

Cø / N* turns clinical and biological cases into structured hypotheses. Repeated assessment, task-based neuroimaging, perturbational measures, and no-report contrasts can provide anchors. They do not independently validate the complete theory. A measure may support one work package—such as boundary stability, availability, or bundle discrimination—without establishing constitutive identity.

### Fair rival comparison

A report-centered clinical classifier and Cø / N* may agree in an alert communicating patient but diverge in locked-in syndrome or an invalid task window. That divergence is fair only if the report-centered model actually treats report as necessary; many workspace and higher-order theories allow consciousness without immediate motor output. Compare a named version and the same target. Similarly, a perturbational-complexity account may issue a state-level prediction where Cø / N* issues a content-specific one. A disagreement requires matched grain and interval rather than two labels placed side by side.

The strongest comparison asks each view to predict a near miss prospectively: motor output lost while the internal bundle persists; task performance absent because the sensory route fails; or an N* component selectively altered while arousal and the candidate remain stable. Both sides should state what invalidates the case and what result would revise their view.

The deepest limit is the absence of a universal consciousness oracle. Investigators rely on converging, partly theory-laden evidence. That does not make testing pointless; it makes independence, preregistration, held-out validation, and calibrated abstention especially important.

Clinical action introduces another layer. A scientific result combines evidence and inference rules. A practical recommendation also combines possible harms, reversibility, urgency, alternatives, and values. The same indeterminate scientific result can rationally support low-burden communication trials or continued monitoring without being relabeled “probably conscious” in the scientific record.

**Do not infer.** Nothing in this chapter is a clinical protocol or treatment recommendation. Classroom cases train inference, not bedside decision-making.

## Chapter summary

- Biological and clinical applications require a fixed order: target, candidate, boundary, viability, N* components, conflict rules, model output, then practical action.
- Positive, negative, indeterminate, and unlicensed are different results, not degrees on one scale.
- Waking report-rich adults are valuable calibration cases but do not define consciousness.
- Sleep, anesthesia, injury, and split systems require time-indexed and target-specific conclusions.
- Motor silence, task failure, and low scores can arise from auxiliary failures rather than absent phenomenal presence.
- Repeated sampling and selective interventions are more informative than one global contrast.
- Empirical classification must remain separate from clinical and ethical recommendation.

## Key terms

**Anchor evidence:** evidence used to evaluate a model prediction without simply reusing the model's own defining measure.  
**Cognitive motor dissociation:** evidence of command-related cognitive activity despite absent bedside motor response.  
**Four-way outcome:** positive, negative, indeterminate, or unlicensed.  
**Negative license:** the validity and fair-opportunity conditions required before non-detection can support absence.  
**Operating regime:** the relevant state conditions, such as drug, arousal, task, or sleep stage.  
**Repeated assessment:** sampling a variable state and test across multiple suitable windows.  
**Target-specific conclusion:** a result restricted to the content, capacity, candidate, and interval actually tested.

# Chapter 12 — Nonhuman Animals, Organoids, and Artificial Systems

## Opening puzzle: the same score in three worlds

An adult human, an octopus, and a neuron–computer organoid hybrid receive the same numerical score on a perturbational-complexity measure. A tool-using artificial agent receives a mathematically similar score from a network analysis. May we carry the human interpretation across all four cases?

Numerical equality feels objective, but the meaning of a number depends on what was measured, what causal role the measured feature plays, how the test was calibrated, and which system the score belongs to. A thermometer reading of 38 can mean a fever in degrees Celsius, an ordinary afternoon in degrees Fahrenheit, or nothing at all if the sensor is measuring a circuit board. Cross-substrate consciousness research faces the same interpretive problem, amplified by the absence of a universal gold standard.

**Plain language.** “Substrate-independent” does not mean “anything with the right-looking score qualifies.” It means that biology is not required by definition if the relevant causal organization can be shown in another material.

## Chapter map

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. explain **constrained substrate independence**;
2. perform a four-part transport audit covering construct, causal role, calibration, and boundary/regime;
3. distinguish neural chauvinism from empty functionalism;
4. analyze animals, infants, organoids, hybrid systems, and artificial agents without importing human assumptions silently;
5. separate a transport license from a Cø / N* verdict;
6. distinguish constituent, interface, enabling support, and environment; and
7. recommend proportional precautions without turning policy into evidence.

## 1. Constrained substrate independence

Cø / N* is stated in causal-dynamical terms. It does not define phenomenal presence as “having human neurons.” In principle, a nonhuman or nonbiological system could realize viability $V$, integration $N_1$, system-wide causal availability $N_2$, and recurrent stability $N_3$. But the words “in principle” do not supply an empirical mapping.

The theory therefore occupies a middle position between two errors:

- **Neural chauvinism:** denying phenomenal presence to any non-neural system without asking whether the same relevant causal roles are realized.
- **Empty functionalism:** granting phenomenal presence whenever an abstract diagram, behavior, or input–output function resembles a conscious case.

The proposed organization must be actually realized in the running system. Investigators must preserve relevant directionality, effective coupling, temporal grain, recurrent organization, recipient structure, operating ranges, and a substrate-appropriate form of viability. **Effective connectivity** means modeled directed influence—how changing one part would change another—not merely an anatomical link or statistical correlation.

Imagine moving a musical score from piano to string quartet. The melody can survive a change of instrument, but only if the new arrangement preserves the relevant relations of pitch, timing, harmony, and phrasing. Copying the page numbers or matching the total number of notes would not be enough.

**Where the analogy breaks.** A musical work has public performance criteria and human judges who already understand its identity. The phenomenal interpretation of a causal role is itself under investigation, so transport requires independent empirical support rather than aesthetic recognition.

**Why it matters.** A theory that permits every convenient mapping cannot be tested. A theory that forbids every unfamiliar realization by definition cannot discover anything surprising.

## 2. The four supports of a transport bridge

Let $T$ be a declared mapping from a source-domain realization to a target-domain realization. The source might be a human neural case in which a measure has some validation. The target might be an octopus, infant, organoid, or artificial agent. Four supports must hold.

### 2.1 Construct preservation

Does the target measure estimate the same property? A human EEG complexity index may combine anatomical, dynamical, and measurement assumptions that do not hold for an organoid or silicon network. Even when the same formula can be computed, the construct may change.

### 2.2 Causal-role preservation

Does the measured feature make the relevant kind of difference inside the target? For $N_2$, apparent “broadcast” must support content-specific influence over multiple functionally different recipient classes. For $N_3$, repeated activity must contribute to recurrent stability rather than merely reflect an externally clocked loop. Homologous anatomy can help, but causal-role evidence is ultimately the important link.

### 2.3 Calibration preservation

Do thresholds, error rates, rule-in regions, rule-out regions, and uncertainty estimates remain defensible? A threshold chosen in healthy adults may fail in a species with different timescales or in hardware with nearly noiseless transmission. Retuning after seeing a favorable case creates an illusion of transport.

### 2.4 Boundary and regime preservation

Which target system receives the attribution, at what grain and horizon? For an organoid, candidates may include tissue alone, tissue plus electrodes, or tissue plus an adaptive controller. For an AI service, candidates may include one model call, the model plus context, an agent loop, a distributed service, or a human–machine team. The mapping must also survive relevant operating-regime differences.

**Method note.** Transport is a license condition, not a fifth consciousness outcome. First ask whether the source interpretation can be applied. Only then use the ordinary positive, negative, indeterminate, or unlicensed rules on target evidence.

A transport bridge resembles an actual bridge with four load-bearing supports. A roadway may look intact from above, yet removal of one pier makes crossing unsafe. Likewise, construct similarity without calibration—or calibration without a stable boundary—does not license the inference.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Physical bridge failure is often binary and directly observable. Transport supports admit degrees, uncertainty, and interacting failures; investigators may need to report which support is weakest rather than declare one dramatic collapse.

**Checkpoint.** Return to the opening puzzle. For each target, identify the transport support most likely to require new evidence before the identical score can be interpreted.

## 3. Animals and infants: ecological fairness

Human-designed tasks often assume human sensory priorities, motor capacities, motivation, and training tolerance. An animal's failure can therefore reflect an unfair interface rather than absence of a target capacity. An octopus may use touch and chemical sensing differently from a primate. A bird may express flexible control through navigation rather than manual response. An infant may lack language and stable motor output while still supporting rich sensory organization. Multidimensional animal-consciousness frameworks help expose these task and capacity mismatches (Birch et al., 2020).

Constructing recipient classes for $N_2$ must be species- and stage-appropriate. Candidate recipients might include orienting, memory updating, value-guided action, internal regulation, or flexible cross-modal learning. Investigators should preregister why the classes are functionally distinct rather than merely count anatomical sites.

Evidence can draw on several sources:

- conserved mechanisms where genuine homology exists;
- causal interventions and pathway-specific effects;
- flexible behavior under species-appropriate conditions;
- motivational tradeoffs and generalization;
- known sensory and motor dependencies;
- perturbational and physiological measures; and
- developmental patterns that support or weaken a proposed bridge.

None of these alone is decisive. The goal is a convergent, independently disciplined mapping.

### Thought experiment: The alien aquarium

Researchers encounter an aquatic organism whose nervous system has no familiar cortex. It learns complex spatial routes, flexibly trades safety against food, and changes internal processing after a content-specific intervention.

**Stage 1.** A human-calibrated cortical marker is absent. Is that a negative result or an out-of-domain test?

**Stage 2.** A different dynamical measure reveals integration and recurrence but was fitted after researchers saw the organism's behavior. What circularity risk appears?

**Stage 3.** A preregistered intervention shows that one content can modulate navigation, memory updating, and defensive control through distinct routes. Which part of N* does this most directly bear on, and what remains untested?

**Do not infer.** Species-appropriate adaptation is not permission to make every criterion easy to pass. The new operationalization must still create risky failures and validated negative regions.

## 4. Organoids and hybrid systems: where does the candidate end?

Neural organoids can display electrical activity, oscillations, complexity, plasticity, and responses to perturbation. Those achievements are scientifically interesting. Yet measures developed in organisms may rely on differentiated anatomy, sensory history, vascular and regulatory support, or behavior that an organoid lacks. Work on embodied neural cultures and organoid intelligence motivates these questions without settling phenomenal attribution (Kagan et al., 2022; Smirnova et al., 2023).

Before asking whether an organoid has Cø, ask what the candidate system is. Suppose tissue is connected to electrodes and an adaptive digital controller. Several possibilities arise:

1. **Tissue-only candidate:** the controller is a replaceable interface or support.
2. **Hybrid candidate:** tissue and controller jointly realize indispensable roles.
3. **Controller-dominated assembly:** the digital system imposes the trajectory, leaving the tissue insufficiently autonomous.
4. **Boundary-indeterminate case:** available interventions cannot distinguish the possibilities.

Removal, matched replacement, latency perturbation, and generic substitution help. If a controller can be replaced by any device delivering the same simple input, it looks more like an interface. If its specific internal memory and feedback are indispensable to role completeness, it may be constitutive at the declared horizon. If it scripts every trajectory, the assembly may fail the autonomy conditions needed for a candidate.

**Plain language.** A wire connected to a system is not automatically part of the subject. Neither is an external service automatically outside it. Constitution is a causal question indexed to time and task.

### Thought experiment: The organoid's borrowed memory

An organoid produces adaptive responses only when a digital controller stores the previous ten trials.

**Stage 1.** Remove the controller; the adaptive pattern vanishes. Does indispensability alone show that the controller is a constituent?

**Stage 2.** Replace it with a simple matched memory buffer; performance returns. How does this affect the boundary hypothesis?

**Stage 3.** Extend the horizon from one second to one day. The controller now maintains long-term state that no generic buffer preserves. Should the candidate family change, and what must be frozen before testing consciousness-relevant properties?

**Method note.** Boundary results are not consciousness verdicts. They identify the $S$ for which $V$ and the $N_i$ conditions can later be assessed.

## 5. Artificial systems: inspect the running architecture

Product labels such as “large language model,” “agent,” or “multi-agent system” are too coarse. A deployed service may include a base model, prompt, current context, retrieval system, persistent memory, scheduler, tool interfaces, evaluators, other models, monitoring software, and human operators. Different subsets may form candidates at different horizons—or none may meet the boundary criteria.

A stateless model call can perform a complex transformation while lacking persistence across calls. An agent loop can add recurrence, memory, and wide availability yet remain externally scripted. A multi-agent service may contain several semi-autonomous candidates, one larger workspace-like candidate, or a collection held together only by a scheduler.

Useful interventions include:

- resetting hidden or persistent state;
- substituting memory stores;
- ablating schedulers or tools;
- delaying or interrupting message routes;
- removing human selection and repair;
- replacing services with matched generic controllers;
- intervening on latent or token-level content; and
- testing whether results survive replication and implementation changes.

Fluent language is not a substitute for these tests. Nor are logs, mirrored replicas, idle subscribers, or large node counts automatically evidence of functional diversity or integration. Theory-derived indicator proposals for artificial systems are best read as research programs with explicit assumptions, not turnkey consciousness detectors (Butlin et al., 2023).

**Why it matters.** Artificial systems are unusually useful construct laboratories. Components can sometimes be manipulated with precision, allowing researchers to test whether proposed $N_i$ measures distinguish the intended causal roles. Success validates construct behavior, not phenomenal presence by itself.

An analogy is a theater production. The audience sees one coherent performance, but the causal organization may include actors, stage manager, lighting board, script, understudies, and remote producer. A polished show does not tell us which parts form an autonomous production unit. Removing and replacing parts reveals the organization.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Theater roles are designed by humans and interpreted against an explicit plan. Artificial systems can acquire unplanned dependencies, and the consciousness-relevant causal roles remain theoretical proposals rather than occupational descriptions.

### Thought experiment: The day-long agent

An AI assistant seems consistent across a day. It uses a base model, retrieval service, persistent memory, scheduler, tools, and periodic human approval.

**Stage 4.** The system verbally claims to be conscious after a prompt requests such a claim. How much evidential weight should that carry without content-specific causal and calibration evidence?

## 6. Artificial viability is a research problem

Biological viability involves maintaining an organized living regime, but an artificial analogue cannot simply be assumed. Several candidates should be compared prospectively:

| Candidate criterion | What it captures | A likely error |
|---|---|---|
| Uptime | continued execution | includes passive scripts maintained from outside |
| Endogenous fault recovery | restoration after bounded disruption | may include a narrow retry service |
| Operating-variable regulation | maintenance of timing, memory, resources, or state integrity | may be supplied entirely by a platform |
| Functional-organization maintenance | preservation of the relevant causal roles | may depend on constant operator reconstruction |
| Goal-preserving adaptation | flexible recovery of task organization | sophisticated control need not be phenomenal |

The choice affects the core inference because $V$ is independently required. It cannot be defined as “whatever keeps a favored AI candidate in the positive set.” Researchers should state the criterion, test it in nonconscious benchmark systems, and explore how it behaves under bounded perturbation.

**Checkpoint.** Propose one artificial system that uptime would falsely include and one adaptive system that a biology-specific viability criterion would falsely exclude.

## 7. Evidence, limits, and transport outcomes

Transport can fail in several informative ways. The construct may not survive; the causal role may differ; thresholds may lack calibration; or the candidate boundary may remain unresolved. These are not equivalent and should be recorded separately.

When transport is licensed, target evidence can still be positive, negative, or indeterminate under Cø / N*. When transport fails, the target application is unlicensed. In addition, a theory-conditional positive can coexist with field-level model indeterminacy if serious rival theories interpret the preserved organization differently.

This layered reporting protects two facts at once: a theory can issue a clear prediction, and the broader field may remain divided about its interpretation.

The principal limitation is that cross-substrate validation is young and case-specific. A detailed protocol is not evidence that a bridge has already been built. Source-domain support, replication, and held-out transport must be distinguished. The farther a target is from the calibration domain, the more explicit the mapping burden becomes.

### Fair rival comparison

Two comparison dimensions are useful even before an exact rival is selected. A **biology-essentialist dimension** asks whether a feature of living organization is constitutive rather than one realization of viability and the N* roles. A **liberal computational-functionalist dimension** asks whether an abstract transition structure is sufficient where Cø / N* instead demands realized causal dynamics, calibrated operating ranges, and a boundary audit. These are dimensions, not complete theory versions. An adversarial study must attach each to a named, sourced view and specify both the biological requirement and the level of computational equivalence.

A fair diagnostic case would preserve the strongest rival's requirements while varying the disputed condition: a nonbiological system with prospectively demonstrated causal-role preservation but no metabolism, or two behaviorally equivalent systems that differ in autonomy and effective coupling. Both views must declare how transport failure, measurement failure, and genuine theoretical challenge are distinguished.

## 8. Precaution without epistemic inflation

Animals, organoids, and artificial systems raise ethical questions before science becomes decisive. Proportional precaution may consider possible severity, reversibility, alternatives, monitoring burden, evidence strength, and uncertainty. Low-cost safeguards can be reasonable under indeterminacy.

But action and evidence must remain separate. A laboratory might limit a potentially harmful organoid protocol because the downside is serious and the safeguard is cheap. That decision does not convert an unlicensed consciousness assessment into a positive one. Conversely, an unlicensed test does not prove that no protection is warranted.

**Do not infer.** “Treat as if conscious for this policy” and “scientifically classified as conscious” are different statements with different premises.

# Chapter 13 — Differentiation, Validation, and the Strongest Objections

## Opening puzzle: a theory made of familiar parts

Integration is central to integrated information theories. Global availability is central to workspace theories. Recurrence is central to recurrent processing theories. Viability appears in biological and dynamical approaches. If Cø / N* combines these familiar ideas, is it a new theory or only a list?

The question cannot be answered by counting novel nouns. A scientific proposal can be distinctive because it assigns familiar elements a new division of labor, joins them under restrictive rules, or predicts a pattern that none of its sources requires. But mere packaging is not enough. The conjunction must generate risky contrasts, disciplined measurements, and revision under failure.

**Plain language.** Novelty is not a certificate issued for unusual vocabulary. The important question is whether the architecture makes a difference to what we should observe and what would force us to change our minds.

## Chapter map

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. state the distinctive commitments of Cø / N* without claiming that every component is new;
2. compare exact versions of rival theories on matched targets;
3. distinguish validation branches from a single all-or-nothing score;
4. classify support by maturity level;
5. steelman major objections and identify their residual pressure;
6. distinguish progressive revision from result-driven rescue; and
7. propose observations that would meaningfully raise or lower confidence.

## 1. What the package commits us to

The distinctive claim lies in a set of joint restrictions:

1. The explanandum is minimal phenomenal presence, not report, intelligence, self-reflection, or access as ordinarily defined.
2. One frozen candidate system must realize every required role for the same target in the same interval.
3. Integration $N_1$, report-independent system-wide causal availability $N_2$, and recurrent stability $N_3$ are jointly necessary and individually insufficient.
4. Viability $V$ is assessed independently and cannot be repaired after seeing the desired verdict.
5. Unresolved credible conflict blocks positive or negative certainty rather than disappearing into a score.
6. The biconditional can fail in two directions: a resolved inclusion-minimal all-pass bearer accompanied by strong, licensed independent evidence against $C_0$ threatens sufficiency; a well-supported $C_0$ case whose recomputed frozen positive-bearer set excludes $S$ threatens necessity.
7. Every conjunct must compete with leave-one-out and grouped rivals on cases where their predictions differ.
8. Phenomenal character is a second layer requiring a positive presence gate and an independently tested bridge.
9. Boundary selection, transport, and schematic legibility are separate work packages with explicit licenses.

That collection is not what IIT, global neuronal workspace theory (GNWT), or recurrent processing theory (RPT) individually asserts. Yet this difference on paper matters only if it creates diagnostic cases.

**Why it matters.** A conjunction can look precise while remaining empirically idle. If the proposed components always rise and fall together, no study can determine whether all are needed.

Think of a four-lock vault. The manufacturer claims that opening requires the correct key for every lock. Observing all four unlocked whenever the vault is open does not prove that each lock is necessary; one may be decorative or mechanically linked to another. We need cases in which one lock can be tested while the others remain correctly set.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Vault opening is publicly observable and the mechanism can be inspected directly. Phenomenal presence lacks a universal external indicator, so tests require held-out anchors and remain vulnerable to anchor assumptions.

## 2. Compare theories only after matching the target

Theory comparisons often become slogan contests: “IIT is integration,” “GNWT is global broadcasting,” or “RPT is recurrence.” Each family contains multiple versions, and neighboring theories may explain different targets. One may aim at phenomenal presence, another at conscious access, another at metacognitive report, another at the structure of experience, and another at the concept of consciousness itself.

Before comparing, prepare a **version card**:

- exact explanandum;
- proposed bearer;
- necessary and sufficient commitments;
- role of report;
- temporal commitment;
- boundary assumptions;
- source passage; and
- prospective prediction for the current case.

Then follow a comparison protocol:

1. Match targets or make the bridge between them explicit.
2. Freeze the theory versions and candidate boundaries.
3. Identify an ordinary agreement case.
4. Build a near miss on which commitments diverge.
5. State observations, validity requirements, and revision rules for both sides.
6. Score the result without retuning either theory.

**Do not infer.** If a GNWT version addresses conscious access and Cø / N* addresses minimal presence, different labels for one case do not yet create a contradiction. The link between access and presence must itself be specified.

### Thought experiment: The three near misses

Consider three idealized candidates, all viable and measured with adequate validity.

**Case A: The integrated island.** Activity is strongly synergistic and recurrent but remains causally isolated from diverse recipient functions.

**Case B: The reducible broadcast.** A content reaches many recipient classes, yet the effects can be decomposed into independent channels with little integration.

**Case C: The recurrent alcove.** A stable local sensory loop persists but does not gain broad causal availability.

For each case:

1. What does Cø / N* predict?
2. Which exact version of IIT, GNWT, or RPT might disagree?
3. Is the apparent disagreement about the same explanandum?
4. What observation would adjudicate without using either theory's output as the ground truth?

**Checkpoint.** If your answer begins “IIT would say...,” add the exact version and target before continuing.

## 3. Fair rival lenses

The following comparisons identify pressure points, not final verdicts.

### Integrated information approaches

IIT-style theories emphasize intrinsic causal structure, complexes, integration, and exclusion. In this vocabulary, a **complex** is the candidate substrate selected by the theory's maximally irreducible cause–effect criterion; **exclusion** requires a definite substrate and grain rather than treating every overlapping candidate as a simultaneous experience. Cø / N* overlaps in treating reducible organization as insufficient, but it adds separate requirements for functionally diverse availability and bounded recurrence. A candidate that clears an IIT-specific intrinsic-structure criterion while remaining local or temporally unstable may therefore divide the theories. Fair comparison requires the exact IIT formulation, its complex-selection rule, and matched boundaries (Albantakis et al., 2023).

### Global workspace and global neuronal workspace approaches

Workspace theories emphasize global availability or ignition. Cø / N* shares concern for widespread causal availability but defines $N_2$ without requiring report or a privileged executive recipient. It also separately requires synergy and recurrence. A case with broad report-free reach but low $N_1$ is a useful contrast. Yet “global” and “available” must be operationalized in both theories rather than assumed equivalent (Mashour et al., 2020).

### Recurrent processing approaches

RPT versions often treat recurrent sensory processing as central and, on some formulations, sufficient for phenomenal consciousness. Cø / N* treats recurrence as necessary but insufficient without integration and diverse availability. A stable local loop with weak reach is therefore an important near miss. The temporal grain and meaning of recurrence must be held constant across the comparison (Lamme, 2006).

### Higher-order theories

Higher-order theories connect consciousness to a suitable representation of a first-order state. Cø / N* does not require explicit metarepresentation for minimal presence. A system satisfying N* without a licensed higher-order state could differentiate them—but only if the higher-order theory really targets the same minimal explanandum (Lau & Rosenthal, 2011).

### Predictive, active-inference, embodied, and enactive approaches

These families often emphasize hierarchical prediction, action-oriented regulation, embodiment, or organism–environment coupling. Cø / N* can include predictive and bodily dynamics inside its components and candidate boundary, but it does not currently make them separate universal conjuncts. Evidence that affective regulation or organismic coupling is repeatedly necessary in otherwise diagnostic cases could motivate revision.

### Attention-schema, illusionist, and fundamentalist views

Attention-schema accounts may explain a system's model of attention and its self-attributions (Graziano & Webb, 2015). Illusionist views may reject phenomenal properties as ordinarily conceived (Frankish, 2016). Panpsychist or other fundamentalist views may locate experience at a more basic metaphysical level. These positions may disagree with Cø / N* about explanandum or metaphysics rather than a case-level neural prediction. Forcing all of them into one score table creates false precision.

**Method note.** A fair comparison can end with “no direct diagnostic contrast at this level.” That is more informative than pretending that every theory predicts a different brain signal.

## 4. Validation is a branching map

It is tempting to imagine one ladder from concept to proof. The Cø / N* program is better represented as a common foundation with several branches.

The common foundation includes conceptual clarity, construct separability, measurement validity, and a sufficiently stable candidate boundary. After that, different questions require different evidence:

| Branch | Main achievement | What failure need not destroy |
|---|---|---|
| Bundle attribution | Licensed $V\land N^*$ profile and positive-bearer result for a case | The target concept or untested operationalizations |
| Component necessity | Full model beats proper submodels on diagnostic cases | Other components and some bundle-level findings |
| Causal intervention | Selective perturbation and rescue alter independent anchors | Correlational construct evidence |
| Transport | Source-domain interpretation survives a new domain | The source-domain result |
| Character bridge | Causal geometry predicts held-out phenomenal relations after the presence gate | Presence classification |
| Schematic competence | Trained readers reconstruct, abstain, and fault-localize reproducibly | Empirical truth of the identity claim |

Failure propagates selectively. If the character bridge fails, the presence gate may remain viable. If one component proves redundant, the exact factorization must change, but the other constructs may retain value. If transport to organoids fails, a human source-domain result is not automatically erased.

This map resembles a house with a common foundation and several wings. A cracked conservatory does not show that the kitchen is unsupported, but a failed foundation threatens every wing that depends on it.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Research branches are not physically separate. Evidence and assumptions can overlap, and a problem in one branch may indirectly alter another through revised constructs or boundaries.

### Maturity levels

Each branch can be described without hype:

1. **Not yet attempted:** a criterion or protocol exists only as a proposal.
2. **Construct demonstration:** a measure behaves as intended in synthetic or tightly controlled cases.
3. **Single-domain support:** frozen predictions succeed in one relevant domain.
4. **Replicated support:** independent teams reproduce the diagnostic result.
5. **Transported support:** construct, causal role, calibration, and boundary/regime survive a materially different domain.

A detailed protocol can improve testability while remaining at the proposal stage. The existence of equations or preregistration fields is not evidence that nature cooperates.

**Plain language.** A blueprint is valuable, but it is not a finished building.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Scientific programs are revised through interaction with evidence; a blueprint normally specifies the object before construction. In research, even the intended structure may change.

## 5. The strongest objections

Good objections identify a route by which the theory could appear successful without earning its conclusion. Each reply below therefore ends with residual pressure.

### 5.1 “This is a hybrid without novelty”

**Reply.** The exposed conjunction, fixed inference order, boundary-first procedure, four-way evidence policy, component audit, and separate character bridge create constraints not jointly imposed by source theories.

**Residual pressure.** The architecture must yield unique diagnostic predictions. If every apparent success is already required by one source theory, or if the conjunction never separates empirically, the novelty claim weakens.

### 5.2 “N₂ smuggles access consciousness back in”

**Reply.** $N_2$ is defined as internal, content-specific, counterfactual causal availability to multiple functionally diverse recipients. Report and executive recipient classes are not required.

**Residual pressure.** If every credible operationalization ultimately relies on report, working memory, or executive control, the distinction may be verbal rather than empirical.

### 5.3 “Viability is an escape hatch”

**Reply.** $V$ must be defined and assessed independently before the critical classification. Versioned criteria and nonconscious benchmark systems can reveal overinclusion.

**Residual pressure.** Viability may differ greatly across substrates. Repeated case-by-case adjustment can protect the theory from counterexamples and damage transport credibility.

### 5.4 “Thresholds manufacture the answer”

**Reply.** Explicit thresholds are better than hidden judgment. They should be calibrated, preregistered, and stress-tested across a specification neighborhood.

**Residual pressure.** If modest threshold changes repeatedly reverse conclusions, the sharp biconditional may need a probabilistic reformulation.

### 5.5 “The evidence is correlation, not constitution”

**Reply.** Selective interventions, route interruption, mechanism ablation, rescue, and independent held-out anchors can move beyond passive decoding.

**Residual pressure.** Even causal relevance does not logically establish metaphysical identity. The constitutive interpretation remains an integrative theoretical judgment.

### 5.6 “The hard problem remains”

**Reply.** The theory offers a mid-level constitutive hypothesis and a compositional account of unity, system-level presence, and temporal presence. It does not claim to derive why any physical organization is accompanied by experience from premises acceptable to every metaphysics.

**Residual pressure.** A conceivability or explanatory-gap objection remains live. Schematic intelligibility does not close it by itself.

### 5.7 “Embodiment and affect are underplayed”

**Reply.** Bodily and affective organization can enter candidate boundaries, viability, content dynamics, and the character profile.

**Residual pressure.** If licensed diagnostic cases show that organismic regulation or valence is independently necessary, the current conjunction may omit a component.

### 5.8 “The conjuncts cannot be separated”

**Reply.** The ablation framework includes a “causally coupled” outcome. It does not force an indispensable verdict when selective dissociation is impossible.

**Residual pressure.** Stable inseparability would undermine claims that the components have separately established necessity. The factorization may need to combine terms.

### 5.9 “There is no consciousness oracle”

**Reply.** Comparative predictions can be tested with plural, held-out anchors that are independently validated and not dual-used to define the model.

**Residual pressure.** Results remain anchor-relative. No finite anchor set eliminates the other-minds problem or every theoretical assumption.

**Checkpoint.** Choose the objection you think creates the greatest empirical risk. What result—not merely argument—would sharpen it?

## 6. Revision rules: change that increases constraint

A research program becomes progressive when failure leads to a principled change that creates more demanding future predictions. Possible revisions include:

- remove a redundant conjunct;
- combine components that are robustly coupled;
- narrow a transport claim;
- replace an invalid operationalization;
- reformulate a brittle sharp threshold probabilistically;
- add a component demanded by repeated diagnostic cases; or
- reject the bundle if licensed counterexamples persist.

A degenerating response moves boundaries after results, shifts thresholds without independent calibration, adds one-off exceptions, or ensures that no future result can count against the view.

### Thought experiment: The moving target laboratory

A preregistered study finds a viable, integrated, recurrent candidate with low $N_2$ and strong positive anchors. The result challenges $N_2$ necessity.

**Stage 1.** The team raises the N* threshold for every component so the case becomes “unlicensed.” Is this a principled revision?

**Stage 2.** The team discovers that the $N_2$ intervention also changed the candidate boundary. Which inference is blocked, and what follow-up becomes necessary?

**Stage 3.** Several independent studies find adequate selectivity and the same pattern. What revision options preserve maximum empirical content?

**Stage 4.** The team proposes a new recipient class after inspecting every dataset. What prospective constraint would distinguish discovery from rescue?

## 7. Evidence and limits

The strongest case for Cø / N* would not be one high-performing classifier. It would combine construct separability, stable boundary determination, diagnostic component cases, selective intervention and rescue, independent anchors, preregistered conflict rules, replication, and cautious transport. Schematic readability would add explanatory organization, while a separately validated geometry bridge would extend the account toward character.

The current corpus mainly articulates a research architecture, methods, failure modes, and proposed empirical programs. That is a real contribution, but it must not be confused with completed empirical validation. A new theory should be presented at the maturity level its evidence supports.

**Do not infer.** Precision in a manuscript is not precision in a measurement. A formal rule can be fully explicit while its operationalization remains immature.

# Chapter 14 — Capstone Adversarial Research Design

## Opening puzzle: the study that cannot lose

A research team proposes to test whether recurrence is necessary for phenomenal presence. If recurrence and the anchor measures both decline, the team will call recurrence necessary. If neither declines, it will say the intervention was too weak. If recurrence declines but anchors do not, it will say another kind of recurrence must have remained. If anchors decline but the recurrence score does not, it will say the score missed the relevant mechanism. Every outcome receives an explanation, yet none can lower confidence.

The problem is not that explanations after data are always illegitimate. The problem is that the study has no exposed commitment. A strong capstone makes room for failure by deciding in advance which target is tested, where rival predictions differ, how measures are licensed, and what revision follows each outcome.

**Plain language.** A scientific plan should say not only “here is what we hope to find,” but also “here is what we will conclude if we do not find it.”

## Chapter map

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. choose one tractable pressure point in the Cø / N* program;
2. define a target, candidate, grain, regime, interval, and diagnostic contrast;
3. construct fair, prospective predictions for the full model and a serious rival;
4. protect measurement validity and anchor independence;
5. specify selectivity, negative controls, equivalence, uncertainty, and missingness;
6. preregister positive, negative, indeterminate, and unlicensed outcomes;
7. map every result to a revision or next commitment; and
8. organize an adversarial team whose disagreement improves the study.

## 1. Choose a branch, not “consciousness in general”

The common foundation freezes the target, candidate family, constructs, measures, and validity rules. From that frozen foundation, choose one branch:

- case-level presence output;
- component-necessity audit;
- transport audit; or
- schematic-competence audit.

Only a character project has the additional gate:

$$
\text{positive presence output}\longrightarrow\text{phenomenal-character bridge}.
$$

A capstone should not attempt every branch. Good projects find one point where an interpretable result would alter the model. Examples include:

- an $N_2$ diagnostic case that preserves $V,N_1,N_3$;
- a feedforward but duration-matched adversary for $N_3$;
- a grouped ablation asking whether $N_1$ and $N_3$ are jointly redundant;
- a boundary intervention in a biological–digital hybrid;
- transport of one validated surrogate across species;
- a geometry-changing intervention for the character bridge; or
- a blinded reproducibility study of schematic readout.

**Why it matters.** “Does anesthesia eliminate consciousness?” is usually too broad. “Does selective recurrent-feedback interruption remove a specified auditory content while preserving $V,N_1,N_2$ in this candidate and interval?” exposes a narrower and more interpretable contrast.

Choosing a capstone is like selecting one load-bearing joint in a bridge for stress testing. Testing every bolt, cable, and foundation at once may look comprehensive, but if the bridge flexes you will not know which element mattered.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Engineered bridges have known design intentions and public failure criteria. Consciousness constructs and anchors are partly contested, so a capstone must also test whether its instruments mean what the design assumes.

## 2. Freeze the specification

Every project needs a written analysis specification $\Theta$ containing at least:

- exact target and unit of analysis;
- candidate system or frozen candidate family;
- spatial and temporal grain;
- operating regime;
- interval $\tau$;
- content or contrast $c$;
- component definitions and thresholds;
- boundary and viability rules;
- primary and rival models;
- data partitions and analysis order; and
- outcome precedence rules.

Freezing does not mean pretending that the first specification is certainly correct. It means recording a primary specification and a principled neighborhood of alternatives before critical inspection. Sensitivity analysis can then show whether the conclusion is stable or fragile.

**Method note.** The specification should be versioned. Amendments remain visible with dates, reasons, and whether critical data were already inspected.

### Thought experiment: The expanding candidate

A team intervenes on a local network to reduce $N_2$. After the result threatens the theory, it expands the candidate boundary to include a remote control loop that restores availability.

**Stage 1.** Why does retrospective expansion threaten the comparison?

**Stage 2.** Suppose boundary evidence collected before the experiment genuinely favored the larger candidate. How should the team proceed?

**Stage 3.** Suppose the intervention itself changes autonomy and creates a new candidate. Is the result negative, indeterminate, or unlicensed for the original comparison?

**Checkpoint.** Write one specification decision in your project that would be tempting to change after an unfavorable result. Add a prospective rule governing it.

## 3. State full and rival predictions before the result

An adversarial project needs at least one genuine rival. This may be another consciousness theory, a leave-one-out version of N*, a grouped-ablation model, a stimulus-only account, or a boundary alternative.

For each model, state:

1. the same target;
2. the same candidate family where possible;
3. the cases on which predictions diverge;
4. the expected direction and meaningful size of the effect;
5. observations that count as support or challenge;
6. license conditions; and
7. revisions under every outcome.

A weak rival is one chosen to lose—for example, “the null theory says nothing matters.” A serious rival explains ordinary cases and disagrees only in a diagnostic region.

### Worked example: interrupting availability

Suppose a visual content is locally stable and integrated. A reversible route intervention prevents that content from modulating several preregistered recipient classes while preserving source-content identity, measurement quality, $V$, $N_1$, and $N_3$.

- The full model predicts that independently constructed presence-relevant anchors should decline when $N_2$ genuinely fails.
- An $N_2$-ablated rival predicts that those held-out anchors can remain stable.

The interrupted routes cannot also supply the decisive anchors. Report and motor output cannot be the only validators. The study must test whether the candidate boundary or operating regime changed. After intervention, positive-bearer status is recomputed rather than inherited from baseline.

This design resembles a court case in which prosecution and defense agree in advance on the identity of the disputed document, chain-of-custody requirements, and what evidence would bear on authenticity.

**Where the analogy breaks.** Courts must reach practical verdicts under legal standards and deadlines. A scientific study may properly return indeterminate or unlicensed, and its goal is not to defeat an opponent.

## 4. Build an anti-circularity firewall

The greatest design danger is allowing the same evidence to define a component, select diagnostic cases, and validate the theory. That is like writing an exam question and grading it with the student's own answer as the key.

Separate evidence streams:

1. **Development data** help design measures and hypotheses.
2. **Calibration data** establish construct behavior, thresholds, and error properties.
3. **Adjudication data** test the frozen predictions.

For each component, separate the measure family used to construct it from the anchors used to adjudicate its necessity. If literal disjointness is impossible, declare the overlap, run a leave-channel-out analysis, and downgrade the main conclusion if it does not survive. If a recurrence score defines $N_3$, it cannot be the sole evidence that removing $N_3$ changed presence. Held-out anchors should be plural and supported by independent validation genealogies.

**Where the analogy breaks.** In real science, complete independence is rare. Measures can share sensors, preprocessing, task demands, and latent causes. The goal is to map and reduce dependence, not merely place variables in different files.

An independence ledger should ask:

- Does the anchor use the same raw sensor as a predictor?
- Does it share preprocessing or a training label?
- Does it depend on the same report or task path?
- Was it selected after seeing the diagnostic effect?
- Is its interpretation derived from the tested theory?
- Could a common cause move both predictor and anchor?

**Do not infer.** “Multimodal” is not synonymous with “independent.” EEG and fMRI measures may still share task demand, arousal, or a theory-derived selection rule.

## 5. Causal diagrams, selectivity, and controls

Draw a causal diagram containing at least:

- intended component intervention;
- retained components;
- candidate boundary;
- viability;
- independent anchors;
- report and task paths;
- common causes;
- measurement channels; and
- selection into the diagnostic set.

Ask four questions:

1. Can the intervention reach the anchors through a path other than the target component?
2. Can a common cause move both the component estimate and anchors?
3. Does selecting diagnostic cases create collider or selection bias? A **collider** is a variable jointly caused by two other variables; conditioning on it can create a false association between those causes even when none existed before selection.
4. Can the intervention change the bearer, content, regime, or viability itself?

Selectivity is not established because the target score changes more than the others. Retained components need equivalence margins: ranges within which a difference is small enough to count as practically preserved. The study also needs a minimum meaningful target effect, negative controls, and, when possible, pathway-specific rescue.

Negative controls should be chosen for their diagnostic role. A sham intervention may detect nonspecific disturbance. A content control may test selectivity. A matched route may expose general communication loss. A rescue can show that restoring the proposed route, rather than merely waiting, reverses the effect.

### Thought experiment: The clean-looking lesion

A virtual lesion lowers an $N_3$ estimator by 40% and leaves point estimates of $N_1$ and $N_2$ almost unchanged.

**Stage 1.** Confidence intervals for retained components are wide. Has selectivity been established?

**Stage 2.** The lesion also slows every process. Could duration, rather than recurrence topology, explain the anchor change?

**Stage 3.** A duration-matched feedforward control preserves anchors. What does that add?

**Stage 4.** A pathway-specific rescue restores recurrence and anchors but also raises arousal. What additional control is needed?

## 6. Predeclare the full outcome space

The four-way classification applies to projects as well as cases.

- **Favorable/supporting:** the diagnostic contrast appears with adequate validity, selectivity, independence, and coverage in the predicted direction.
- **Disfavoring:** the rival prediction succeeds under equally adequate conditions, or a licensed counterexample challenges necessity or sufficiency.
- **Indeterminate:** valid evidence straddles, credible anchors conflict, or uncertainty remains too large.
- **Unlicensed:** applicability, execution quality, required validity, selectivity, transport needed for a cross-domain claim, or fair detection opportunity fails.

These labels should be project-specific. A null result can mean different things:

- redundancy, if the ablated rival performs equivalently with adequate diagnostic coverage;
- low power or imprecision, if equivalence cannot be established;
- inadequate diagnostic cases, if the models rarely disagree;
- invalid intervention, if selectivity fails; or
- genuine indeterminacy, if valid evidence remains conflicted.

Precedence rules matter. If applicability, execution quality, or required validity fails, the assessment is unlicensed. If several independently admissible boundaries or models yield different licensed results, classify the field-level result as indeterminate and record its cause profile. Boundary reversal should not be buried inside ordinary error bars. Anchor conflict should trigger the declared conflict policy rather than an average chosen for convenience.

**Plain language.** A study can fail to answer its question for a specific, informative reason. Naming that reason is better science than forcing every dataset into “worked” or “did not work.”

## 7. Quantitative readiness without false precision

A graduate capstone need not solve every power problem, but it should be executable. State:

- minimum meaningful effect;
- equivalence margins for retained components;
- expected diagnostic coverage;
- multiplicity plan;
- missing-data rule;
- stopping rule;
- primary model and robustness models;
- specification neighborhood; and
- simulated-data recovery check.

Three terms prevent false precision. An **estimand** is the exact quantity the analysis aims to estimate, such as a mean target-versus-control anchor difference in the diagnostic set. **Cross-fitting** rotates data through training and held-out roles so each final prediction is evaluated on observations not used to fit that prediction. A **multiplicity plan** controls or transparently organizes the extra false-positive opportunities created by testing many outcomes, models, subgroups, or time windows.

Diagnostic coverage deserves special attention. If full and ablated models differ on only 2% of observations, excellent average accuracy may conceal a useless necessity test. Report how many cases actually enter the comparison region and how selection affects generalization.

Missingness can also be causal. Artifact may increase precisely when a patient is unstable or an intervention is strongest. A complete-case analysis can therefore select an unrepresentative subset. Record missingness reasons and sensitivity analyses prospectively.

**Method note.** A simulation does not validate the theory. It tests whether the planned analysis can recover known patterns and classify the declared outcomes under controlled conditions.

## 8. Adversarial collaboration is governance

Good will is not enough. Proponent and rival representatives need symmetric authority over prediction statements, controls, exclusions, and result language. Use a disagreement log. If agreement cannot be reached, preregister both analyses or report the unresolved dispute.

Useful roles include:

- **Principal theorist:** reconstructs Cø / N* and protects target fidelity.
- **Rival representative:** ensures the alternative is serious and fairly tested.
- **Causal-design lead:** freezes candidates and maps pathways and common causes.
- **Statistics and reproducibility lead:** specifies effects, equivalence, coverage, missingness, code, and data structure.
- **Independence and licensing auditor:** tracks evidence partitions, dual use, precedence, and transport.

Blinding, scripted analysis, version control, and locked stopping rules can reduce result-driven negotiation. The rival need not endorse Cø / N*. The fairness agreement says only that the test would bear on the declared contrast if its validity conditions pass.

## 9. From every outcome to a next commitment

An outcome-to-revision table is the capstone's intellectual center. For every cell, distinguish:

- theory challenge;
- construct-operationalization failure;
- intervention-selectivity failure;
- anchor-validity failure;
- boundary reversal;
- transport failure; and
- inconclusive precision or coverage.

Then state the next commitment. A licensed challenge to $N_2$ necessity may require removing or narrowing the conjunct. A failed $N_2$ measure requires new construct validation, not immediate theory rejection. A changed candidate boundary requires a new boundary-frozen test. A transport failure narrows scope. Repeated licensed sufficiency counterexamples may require rejecting the bundle.

The capstone succeeds when readers can see how a result changes the research program. It need not promise a decisive metaphysical settlement.

## 10. Evidence and limits

Adversarial preregistration cannot eliminate judgment. Target choice, anchor interpretation, equivalence margins, and model versions remain contestable. Selective interventions may be technically impossible in biological systems, and synthetic systems may not transport phenomenally. A clean null can still leave multiple explanations.

The value of the workflow is narrower and substantial: it exposes assumptions, prevents easy reinterpretation, separates failure causes, and makes future disagreement productive. It turns “more research is needed” into a specific statement about what kind of research and why.

**Do not infer.** A preregistered study is not automatically a good study. Preregistration preserves decisions; it cannot repair an invalid construct, unfair rival, or incoherent target.

## Chapter summary

- A strong capstone tests one tractable branch or pressure point.
- Targets, candidate families, thresholds, data partitions, and outcome rules should be frozen and versioned.
- Full and rival models must make prospective predictions on a diagnostic case.
- Development, calibration, and adjudication evidence should be separated.
- Anchor independence requires a dependency audit, not merely multiple modalities.
- Causal diagrams reveal alternative paths, common causes, selection bias, and boundary changes.
- Selectivity requires retained-component equivalence, negative controls, and preferably rescue.
- Favorable, disfavoring, indeterminate, and unlicensed outcomes should all map to declared next commitments.
- Adversarial collaboration needs symmetric governance and an auditable disagreement process.

## Key terms

**Adjudication data:** held-out data used to evaluate frozen predictions.  
**Diagnostic coverage:** the proportion and diversity of cases on which compared models actually disagree.  
**Equivalence margin:** a prospectively justified range within which a retained component counts as practically unchanged.  
**Independence ledger:** a record of shared sensors, preprocessing, labels, tasks, and theoretical assumptions among predictors and anchors.  
**Minimum meaningful effect:** the smallest target change that would matter for the theoretical contrast.  
**Negative control:** a condition expected not to show the target-specific effect but able to reveal a confound.  
**Outcome-to-revision table:** prospective mapping from every declared result class to its inferential consequence and next commitment.  
**Specification neighborhood:** a justified set of nearby analytic choices used to assess robustness.  
**Symmetric authority:** equal procedural input for serious rival and proponent representatives.

## Where inquiry goes next

You now have the conceptual and methodological vocabulary to engage the Cø / N* program as a participant rather than a spectator. The appropriate posture is neither loyalty nor reflexive dismissal. It is disciplined curiosity: reconstruct the claim accurately, identify its strongest pressure point, design the fair test, preserve the limits of the result, and revise in proportion to what the evidence licenses.

# Appendix A — Notation at a Glance

Notation is useful only when it reduces ambiguity. Read every symbol with its local prose label, especially when a letter is reused in different papers. Your first task is not calculation; it is identifying what a symbol commits the investigator to measure, hold fixed, or refrain from inferring.

## The core claim

$$
C_0(S,c,\tau) \leftrightarrow V(S,\tau)\land N_1(S,c,\tau)\land N_2(S,c,\tau)\land N_3(S,c,\tau).
$$

Read this as a **hypothesis**, not a definition: minimal phenomenal presence for candidate $S$, content or content-bearing state $c$, and interval $\tau$ obtains if and only if the same candidate is viable and jointly realizes integration, system-wide causal availability, and recurrent stability in the declared specification.

This is the compact source profile conjecture. It suppresses bearer selection. Empirically, $H_*(S,c)=V\land N^*$ may be estimated only for a frozen $S\in\mathcal P_\Theta$; final subject attribution additionally requires $\operatorname{Resolved}_B(c;\Theta)$ and $S\in\mathcal B_{\min,\Theta}^{+}(c)$. Hatted values are estimates and statuses, not ontic properties themselves.

| Symbol or term | Student reading | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| $C_0$ or Cø | minimal phenomenal presence | not report, intelligence, wakefulness, richness, or self-reflection |
| $C_1$ | enriched reflective or metacognitive consciousness | not required by the minimal gate |
| $S$ | candidate system | must be generated and frozen before the desired verdict |
| $c$ | target content or content-bearing state | conclusions can be content-relative |
| $\tau$ or $\Delta$ | declared interval or horizon | presence and boundaries can be time-indexed |
| $g$ | spatial, functional, and temporal grain | cross-grain comparison needs a mapping |
| $\mathcal R$ | operating regime | includes state, task, drug, arousal, and relevant context |
| $\Theta$ | frozen analysis specification | collects target, candidate rules, grain, interval, estimators, thresholds, and mappings |
| $V$ | independently assessed viability | not biological life alone or an after-the-fact rescue |
| $N_1$ | integration or synergy | not mere activity, correlation, or complexity |
| $N_2$ | report-independent system-wide causal availability | not diffusion, anatomical size, report, or node count |

| $H_*(S,c)$ | bundle profile $V\land N^*$ on a frozen referent | an all-pass profile is not yet final subject attribution if the bearer is unresolved |

## Evidence and outcome notation

| Term | Student reading | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| Positive | evidence licenses attribution of the exact target under the declared model | not certainty or complete explanation |
| Negative | valid, sensitive evidence enters a rule-out region | not simply failure to pass a positive threshold |
| Indeterminate | valid evidence does not discriminate enough | not “half conscious” |
| Unlicensed | an interpretation prerequisite failed | not a subtle negative |
| Anchor vector $Y$ | held-out convergent evidence for model comparison | not an infallible consciousness oracle |
| Surrogate conflict | credible measures disagree beyond a preregistered tolerance | check before aggregation |

Its indeterminacy cause profile is $P_R(\mathcal A)=\langle e,s,m,b,t\rangle$: evidence scarcity, surrogate discordance, model dependence, boundary/grain sensitivity, and transport uncertainty. Neither tuple is a consciousness score.

| Symbol or term | Student reading | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| $\mathcal D_k$ | diagnostic cases where component $k$ fails and every retained component passes | only here do full and leave-one-out conjunctions disagree |
| $\Gamma_k$ | diagnostic coverage in the target distribution | average accuracy can hide poor coverage |
| Criterion ablation | delete a term from the decision rule | tests incremental prediction, not a physical mechanism |
| Mechanism ablation | intervene on a proposed realization | requires selectivity and boundary checks |
| Measurement ablation | remove an estimator or data route | tests measurement robustness, not physical necessity |
| Indispensable | full model reliably beats the declared rival in diagnostic cases | relative to anchors, domain, and specification |
| Redundant | the rival performs equivalently under adequate conditions | do not infer from a nonsignificant difference alone |
| Causally coupled | tested interventions cannot vary components separately | may motivate a composite factorization |

| Symbol or term | Student reading | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| $P(c)$ | presence-gate indicator for content $c$ | a gate, not a richness score |
| $G_c$ | causal-trajectory neighborhood for a content | not an arbitrary latent embedding |
| $\mathcal Q$ | independently anchored empirical quality-space structure | need not be Euclidean or completely labeled |
| $F_\Phi$ | frozen bridge from causal geometry to quality relations | in-sample fit is insufficient |
| $\chi_c$ | structured character profile | not delivered by N* alone |

## Three quantitative habits

1. **Conjunction:** $X_1\land\cdots\land X_k=1$ only if every term passes. Do not average a failure away unless the rival model explicitly proposes compensation.
2. **Intervals:** a point estimate above a threshold is not enough when its uncertainty interval straddles. Use the preregistered lower- and upper-bound rules.
3. **Equivalence:** “not statistically different” does not show that retained components are preserved. The full uncertainty interval should fall inside a justified equivalence margin.

# Appendix B — How to Read an Empirical Claim

An empirical claim has more structure than “the score increased” or “the theory was supported.” Use the following sequence whenever you read a paper, hear a presentation, or write your own result.

## 1. Locate the exact claim

Underline the smallest sentence that states the result. Then identify its grammar:

- **Target:** What property or phenomenon is being attributed?
- **Bearer:** To which system or unit?
- **Scope:** Which population, content, regime, and time window?
- **Contrast:** Compared with what?
- **Modality:** Is the language causal, correlational, predictive, constitutive, or merely descriptive?
- **Qualifier:** Under which model, assumptions, and confidence?

“The classifier detected consciousness in patients” is too compressed. A more auditable form might be: “Under model $M$ and the preregistered validity conditions, the classifier entered the positive region for covert command-following capacity in three high-arousal assessment windows.” The second sentence may sound less dramatic, but it tells readers what was actually tested.

## 2. Identify the inferential branch

Ask which work package the evidence bears on:

- construct separability;
- boundary validity;
- case-level bundle attribution;
- component necessity;
- causal mechanism;
- transport;
- phenomenal-character mapping; or
- schematic competence.

Do not let evidence migrate silently between branches. A synthetic network can show that an $N_2$ estimator distinguishes routing from diffusion. It cannot, by that fact alone, validate phenomenal presence. A successful human character bridge does not automatically transport to an animal or AI.

## 3. Reconstruct the evidence chain

Write the chain in separate lines:

1. **Observation:** what was recorded or manipulated?
2. **Preprocessing:** how was the raw record transformed?
3. **Estimator:** what score or category was produced?
4. **Construct bridge:** why is the estimator interpreted as the target construct?
5. **Model rule:** how does that construct enter the prediction?
6. **Anchor comparison:** what independent evidence evaluates the prediction?
7. **Conclusion:** what is licensed at the stated scope?

Every arrow is a possible failure point. A beautiful final graph does not repair an invalid construct bridge.

## 4. Check validity before direction

Before asking whether a result is positive or negative, ask whether the test had the right to speak. Check:

- target and candidate match;
- boundary and regime are stable;
- population and substrate fall within the validity envelope;
- detection had a fair opportunity;
- recording quality passes;
- intervention selectivity passes;
- calibration and thresholds were frozen;
- conflicts follow a declared policy; and
- missingness and exclusions do not create a favorable subset.

If applicability, execution quality, or required validity fails, classify the inference as unlicensed even if the number looks impressive. If several independently admissible boundaries or models yield different licensed results, classify the field-level result as indeterminate and record the relevant cause profile.

## 5. Separate point estimate, uncertainty, and decision

A point estimate summarizes data under a model. An uncertainty interval represents a set of values compatible with the data and assumptions. A decision rule maps that evidence to an outcome. These are different objects.

Ask:

- Does the full interval clear the rule-in region?
- Does it clear a validated rule-out region?
- Does it straddle a threshold?
- Were equality rules specified?
- Is the conclusion stable across a reasonable specification neighborhood?
- Was equivalence tested or merely a nonsignificant difference reported?

## 6. Audit alternative explanations

Create a two-column ledger. In the left column, write every path by which the intended construct could produce the observation. In the right, write alternative paths: arousal, report, attention, task difficulty, common input, sensor leakage, selection bias, global disruption, boundary change, or retuned thresholds. For each alternative, name a negative control, measurement, or design change that would distinguish it.

## 7. Ask what would count against the claim

A claim with no disfavoring outcome is not yet an exposed empirical claim. Record:

- licensed favorable result;
- licensed disfavoring result;
- indeterminate result;
- license failure;
- required revision under each; and
- stopping condition for repeated repair.

## 8. Keep action separate

Scientific results inform decisions but do not determine them alone. A practical action combines evidence with values, losses, reversibility, burden, and alternatives. Mark each premise accordingly.

**Scientific premise:** ________________________________________

**Value or loss premise:** _____________________________________

**Recommended action:** ________________________________________

**Why the action does not change the scientific label:** _______

# Appendix C — How to Argue Fairly Across Theories

Fair argument is not timid argument. It is exact enough that disagreement bears on the strongest available version of a claim. Use this appendix when leading a seminar, writing a comparative essay, or preparing an adversarial study.

## 1. State the common question

Two theories can use the word “consciousness” while targeting different phenomena. Before comparing them, write:

- Theory A's explanandum: ______________________________________
- Theory B's explanandum: ______________________________________
- Shared target, if any: _______________________________________
- Bridge needed to compare them: _______________________________
- What a direct comparison would leave out: ____________________

If the targets cannot be matched, compare how the theories choose targets rather than pretending they make opposing case-level predictions.

## 2. Reconstruct before criticizing

For each theory, identify an exact version and a source passage. Separate what the source explicitly claims from your inference about its implications. Ask a knowledgeable advocate whether your reconstruction is recognizable.

A useful reconstruction contains:

1. target;
2. bearer;
3. necessary commitments;
4. sufficient commitments;
5. role of report and access;
6. temporal and boundary assumptions;
7. ordinary success cases;
8. diagnostic failure cases; and
9. explicit limitations.

Do not make a rival easier to defeat by replacing it with a family label.

## 3. Apply symmetric standards

If you demand a precise boundary from one theory, demand it from the other. If one view may appeal to measurement error, specify when the rival may do so. If a failed prediction triggers revision rather than rejection for your preferred theory, allow the same option under equally constraining rules for its competitor.

## 4. Distinguish kinds of disagreement

A disagreement may concern:

- **semantics:** what “phenomenal presence” means;
- **metaphysics:** what kind of fact consciousness is;
- **constitution:** which organization is identical with it;
- **mechanism:** how that organization is produced or maintained;
- **measurement:** which observable estimates a construct;
- **evidence policy:** what licenses positive, negative, or abstaining conclusions;
- **scope:** which systems and regimes are covered; or
- **practical action:** what to do under uncertainty.

Label the level before offering evidence. A neural intervention may adjudicate a mechanism disagreement while leaving a metaphysical disagreement intact.

## Discussion norms for difficult topics

- Critique claims, operationalizations, and inferences rather than a person's intelligence or motives.
- Give participants time to formulate objections in writing before rapid discussion.
- Rotate advocacy roles so students learn reconstruction without requiring personal agreement.
- Make uncertainty an acceptable endpoint.
- Do not use patients, disabled people, animals, or disputed artificial systems merely as rhetorical props; preserve the distinction between a teaching case and a real individual.
- Cite the strongest accessible source for a theory version, not only a secondary caricature.
- Correct your own side's overstatement before exploiting an opponent's imprecision.

## Fair-comparison rehearsal

Choose two exact theory versions and form groups of four: one advocate per theory, one target-matching auditor, and one recorder. In six minutes, agree on a single diagnostic case and write four lines:

1. the shared question or the explicit bridge between different questions;
2. each theory's prospective prediction;
3. the validity condition either advocate could fairly invoke; and
4. the result that would leave the comparison unresolved.

Swap the four-line record with another group. The reviewing group may reject it only by naming a mismatched target, a caricatured commitment, an asymmetric license, or a nondiagnostic case. Return one accepted correction and one residual disagreement. This rehearsal is successful when both advocates regard the revised test as risky, not when the class reaches a vote.

# Glossary

**Ablation:** A planned removal used to test a claim. Criterion ablation removes a term from a rule; mechanism ablation intervenes on a proposed realization; measurement ablation removes an estimator or data route. These answer different questions.

**Access consciousness:** Availability of information for reasoning, report, and control in some philosophical and scientific traditions. It must not be assumed identical to minimal phenomenal presence.

**Adversarial collaboration:** A governed project in which advocates of competing views jointly specify predictions, controls, exclusions, and result language before critical data are inspected.

**Anchor evidence:** Held-out, convergent evidence used to evaluate a model prediction. An anchor is not an oracle and should not merely repeat the model's defining input.

**Autonomy:** The degree to which a candidate's organization is endogenously sustained rather than imposed from outside. Autonomy contributes to candidacy; it is not itself consciousness.

**Bearer:** The system to which a phenomenal attribution would apply. The bearer must be resolved rather than supplied by an unexamined pronoun such as “it.”

**Boundary audit:** A prospective procedure for generating, freezing, and stress-testing candidate systems before measuring consciousness-relevant properties.

**Boundary reversal:** A change in classification caused by changing the candidate system. It reveals that individuation is part of the claim.

**Calibration:** The process of relating a measure to independently supported construct behavior, thresholds, uncertainty, and error rates in a declared domain.

**Candidate system:** A causally admissible possible bearer defined at a declared grain, regime, and horizon. Candidacy does not imply a positive consciousness result.

**Causal availability ($N_2$):** The content-specific capacity to modulate multiple functionally different recipient classes through supported routes within a bounded context. It does not require overt report.

**Causal role:** The difference a property makes in the running organization, not merely its anatomical location, name, or correlation.

**Causally coupled:** An audit outcome in which the available interventions cannot vary proposed components separately. It may motivate a composite factorization rather than an indispensability claim.

**Constitutive identity hypothesis:** The proposal that minimal phenomenal presence is identical with viable N* organization in a resolved inclusion-minimal bearer. The biconditional states the hypothesis; it does not validate itself.

**Construct:** A theoretically defined property such as integration or availability that must be connected to observable estimators through validation evidence.

**Construct preservation:** Evidence that a measure estimates the same property after transport to a new domain.

**Content selectivity:** Evidence that an effect depends on the represented content rather than undifferentiated energy, arousal, or diffusion.

**Counterfactual availability:** A capacity revealed by appropriate interventions across nearby admissible contexts even when every recipient is not actually active at once.

**Criterion ablation:** Removal of a term from a decision rule to test incremental prediction.

**Diagnostic case:** A case on which frozen competing models make different predictions.

**Diagnostic coverage:** The amount and diversity of target-domain data that actually fall in a diagnostic region.

**Equivalence margin:** A justified interval within which a retained component counts as practically preserved. Nonsignificance alone is not equivalence.

**Explanandum:** The phenomenon a theory aims to explain. Here the core target is minimal phenomenal presence.

**Four-way outcome:** Positive, negative, indeterminate, or unlicensed. The last two prevent incomplete or invalid evidence from being forced into a binary verdict.

**Functional recipient class:** A group defined by a distinct causal response repertoire. It should not be created merely by splitting an anatomical parcel.

**Grain:** The spatial, functional, and temporal resolution at which a candidate and its dynamics are described.

**Held-out evidence:** Data not used to choose the final measures, thresholds, or models that it later evaluates.

**Inclusion-minimality:** The condition that no qualifying proper subsystem exists at the same specification. It does not mean smallest node count.

**Indeterminate:** A licensed outcome in which relevant evidence remains insufficient, threshold-straddling, or credibly conflicted.

**Integration or synergy ($N_1$):** Joint causal or informational contribution not reducible to independent parts. Activity and statistical correlation are not sufficient.

**License condition:** A prerequisite that determines whether an evidential interpretation is allowed, such as validity, boundary stability, selectivity, or transport.

**Mechanism ablation:** An intervention on a proposed physical or computational realization. It requires selectivity and re-evaluation of boundaries and retained components.

**Measurement ablation:** Removal of a sensor, estimator, or analysis route to test robustness of measurement.

**Minimal phenomenal presence ($C_0$ or Cø):** The minimal fact that there is something it is like for a system in a specified interval. It is not equivalent by definition to report, wakefulness, intelligence, reflection, or richness.

**Model indeterminacy:** Disagreement among serious theories about how to interpret a case, even when evidence within each theory may be decisive.

**N-star ($N^*$):** The conjunction $N_1\land N_2\land N_3$: integration, system-wide causal availability, and recurrent stability.

**Negative:** A licensed rule-out for the exact target based on valid, sensitive, adequate evidence. It is not simply a failed positive test.

**Negative control:** A condition designed to reveal a specific confound because the target-specific effect should not appear there.

**Operating regime:** Relevant state conditions such as arousal, task, drug, developmental stage, temperature, or computational mode.

**Phenomenal character:** What an experience is like—its qualitative organization—rather than the bare fact that experience is present.

**Positive:** A licensed attribution of the exact target under declared model and evidence rules. It is not certainty.

**Positive bearer:** A candidate that clears the role profile and the declared inclusion-minimality and separability conditions needed for final attribution.

**Precaution:** A practical response to possible harm under uncertainty. It combines evidence with loss, value, and reversibility assumptions and is not itself evidence of consciousness.

**Preregistration:** A timestamped record of hypotheses, models, measures, validity rules, analyses, outcomes, and revisions before critical data inspection.

**Recurrent stability ($N_3$):** Bounded recurrent organization that sustains a content-relevant dynamic pattern. It is not equivalent to mere duration or late activity.

**Report-independent:** Not requiring speech, button press, or another overt report as a constitutive criterion. Reports may still contribute as independently calibrated evidence.

**Rescue:** Restoration of a proposed causal route or component used to test whether an observed effect is pathway-specific.

**Rule-in region:** A preregistered evidence region sufficient for a positive model-relative classification under passed licenses.

**Rule-out region:** A validated evidence region sufficient for a negative classification under fair detection conditions.

**Schematic legibility:** A publicly teachable ability to reconstruct bearer, operation, unity, system-level presence, temporal presence, and fault consequences from a role-annotated model. It is intelligibility, not proof.

**Separability:** Preservation of each candidate's organization under reciprocal channel interruption. It helps assess whether plural candidates are distinct.

**Specification neighborhood:** A prospectively justified set of nearby analytic choices used to assess conclusion stability.

**Substrate independence:** The claim that a form of causal organization is not restricted by definition to one material. In this program it is constrained by transport evidence.

**Sufficiency threat:** A resolved inclusion-minimal all-pass bearer with strong, licensed independent negative evidence for $C_0$ at the same target, candidate, regime, and interval.

**Surrogate conflict:** Credible measures of a target that disagree beyond a preregistered tolerance.

**Target:** The precise property or capacity being assessed for a declared bearer, content, regime, and interval.

**Transport:** Application of a source-domain interpretation to a new domain after construct, causal-role, calibration, and boundary/regime preservation are supported.

**Unlicensed:** A meta-level result indicating that a prerequisite for interpretation failed. It is not evidence of absence.

**Validity envelope:** The domain in which the relationship between evidence and target interpretation has been established.

**Version card:** A source-backed record of an exact theory formulation, including target, bearer, commitments, report role, temporal assumptions, and prediction.

**Viability ($V$):** Independently assessed capacity of the candidate to realize the relevant dynamics in the operating regime. It is not a post hoc exception.

# References and Source Trail

This textbook follows a seven-paper project. The list below records the role each paper plays in the shared research architecture. It deliberately avoids invented dates, journal names, volume numbers, or publication claims. Consult the PDFs distributed with the course for their exact internal reference lists and version information.

## 1. *Cø as N\*: A Minimal Network-Dynamics Model of Phenomenal Consciousness*

**Role in the project:** the core hypothesis. It narrows the explanandum to minimal phenomenal presence and proposes that viability plus integration, system-wide availability, and recurrent stability are jointly necessary and sufficient for that target when realized by a resolved inclusion-minimal bearer for the same content and interval. It exposes both necessity and sufficiency failure directions and separates the presence gate from richer reflection and phenomenal character.

**Use this source when:** defining Cø, $V$, $N_1$, $N_2$, $N_3$, the conjunction, the principal applications, or the overall research program.

## 2. *Where Is the Conscious Subject? A Dynamical Criterion for System Boundaries*

**Role in the project:** the scope and bearer condition. It argues that candidate systems should be generated from independent signs of causal autonomy, frozen prospectively, assessed for role completeness, and filtered through inclusion-minimality and separability before final subject attribution. It distinguishes constituents, interfaces, and enabling supports and permits plural or unresolved boundary outcomes.

**Use this source when:** a claim depends on where the system begins and ends, especially for split brains, brain–body loops, coupled agents, organoid–controller hybrids, or distributed artificial systems.

## 3. *Consciousness Without Report: What System-Wide Availability Actually Requires*

**Role in the project:** the detailed operationalization of $N_2$. It distinguishes actual activation from availability, report from causal role, broadcast from diffusion, and functional diversity from node count. It proposes content-specific interventions, independently defined recipient classes, route tests, bounded latency, and report/executive removal audits.

**Use this source when:** analyzing no-report paradigms, locked-in or command-following cases, dream states, posterior or subcortical organization, species differences, or distributed artificial architectures.

## 4. *Indeterminacy as a Scientific Result: A Four-Outcome Framework for Consciousness Attribution*

**Role in the project:** the evidence and reporting discipline. It distinguishes positive, negative, indeterminate, and unlicensed results and decomposes uncertainty into evidential scarcity, surrogate discordance, model disagreement, boundary/grain uncertainty, and transport uncertainty. It also separates scientific status from practical action.

**Use this source when:** a test is invalid, conflicting, underpowered, out of domain, or ethically urgent; when interpreting abstention rates; or when defining outcome precedence.

## 5. *From Phenomenal Presence to Phenomenal Character: A Dynamical-Geometry Extension of the N\* Model*

**Role in the project:** the second-layer account of what experience is like. After a positive presence gate, it proposes mapping content-specific causal-trajectory geometry to independently anchored relations in an empirical quality space. It requires held-out tests, geometry-changing interventions, invariance analysis, and explicit transport licenses.

**Use this source when:** moving from whether experience is present to how color, sound, pain, valence, temporal flow, or cross-modal unity might be structured.

## 6. *Ablating N\*: Does Every Conjunct Earn Its Place?*

**Role in the project:** the component-necessity audit. It explains why a conjunct does not earn necessity merely by covarying with the others. It compares the full model with leave-one-out and grouped rivals on diagnostic cases, separates criterion, mechanism, and measurement ablations, protects held-out anchor independence, and allows indispensable, redundant, causally coupled, indeterminate, and unlicensed outcomes.

**Use this source when:** designing selective interventions, testing minimality, interpreting nulls, measuring diagnostic coverage, or deciding whether failure calls for component removal, combination, or better operationalization.

## 7. *Reading Consciousness from the Schematic: Structural Legibility in the N\* Model*

**Role in the project:** the intelligibility bridge. Using the limited analogy of expert schematic reading, it asks whether a trained reader can reconstruct the bearer, operation, unity, system-level presence, temporal presence, and localized fault consequences from a role-annotated model. It distinguishes conceptual legibility from empirical confirmation.

**Use this source when:** asking whether the proposed identity is explanatorily readable, whether experts can abstain appropriately, or whether fault localization transfers across independently tested cases.

## How the sources fit together

The core paper states the risky identity hypothesis. The boundary paper fixes the candidate system to which it applies. The availability paper makes one conjunct experimentally sharper without defining it by report. The indeterminacy paper governs what evidence licenses. The ablation paper asks whether every conjunct is truly needed. The character paper extends the project beyond bare presence. The schematic paper asks what would make the whole architecture structurally intelligible. No single companion paper independently establishes the core identity, and success in one work package does not automatically validate the rest.
