# Full Audit Report: *Teaching Cø / N\**

**Edition:** 2.1

**Audit date:** July 18, 2026

**Status:** **Pass for controlled graduate-level teaching distribution**

**Residual status:** No known blocking defect; empirical theory validation, specialist accessibility testing, and classroom outcome validation remain open by design.

## 1. Executive assessment

The manual is now a coherent, teachable, and reproducibly packaged account of the seven-paper Cø / N\* research program. It places the proposal among major consciousness theories without reducing those rivals to slogans; distinguishes the source papers' claims from later instructor synthesis; presents every major component with explicit scope, notation, measurement logic, and failure conditions; and turns differentiation and validation into adversarial research tasks rather than declarations of success.

The audit identified and corrected weaknesses in five domains:

1. **Conceptual and formal integration:** the simple component-profile biconditional did not by itself determine the final phenomenal bearer; boundary, ablation, evidence, character, and schematic claims sometimes operated at different units of analysis.
2. **Inferential discipline:** several passages needed clearer distinctions among ontic properties, estimated classifications, positive and negative results, indeterminacy, invalid inference, profile disagreement, and bearer disagreement.
3. **Exercise validity:** some staged cases lacked an interval, used an undefined edge case, disclosed evidence at the wrong stage, exceeded the available class time, or asked teams to infer more than their packet supplied.
4. **Audience safety and reproducibility:** a single reveal master was unsafe for digital distribution; reveal completeness checks were weak; artifact publication could leave a mixed release; and generated files had become stale relative to the sources.
5. **Document quality:** fixed contents references and one wide mathematical pipeline required correction and fresh visual inspection.

All identified release-blocking defects were corrected. The final build passes source, artifact, audience, hash, font, navigation, pagination, and rendering checks. The audit validates the **coherence and teachability of the manual**, not the truth of Cø / N\*.

## 2. Scope and method

### Materials audited

The review covered:

- every page of all three final master PDFs; and
- the repository documentation needed to find and distribute the teaching package safely.

### Audit standards

The manual was tested against six standards:

| Standard | Audit question |
|---|---|
| Corpus coverage | Does the manual incorporate each paper's distinctive contribution rather than teaching only the core formula? |
| Conceptual/formal coherence | Are targets, units, symbols, quantifiers, boundary rules, uncertainty rules, and conclusions mutually consistent? |
| Comparative fairness | Are IIT, GNWT, RPT, higher-order, predictive-processing, recurrent, biological, and eliminativist alternatives compared at matched targets and versioned commitments? |
| Pedagogical alignment | Do objectives, lecture notes, exercises, discussions, evidence releases, keys, and assessments require the reasoning they claim to teach? |
| Audience and accessibility safety | Can students receive usable materials without keys or premature evidence, and are equivalent participation routes supplied? |
| Artifact integrity | Can a reader reproduce the release and verify that its files correspond to the audited source? |

The audit distinguishes four kinds of warrant:

- **source coherence**: the manual's claims and rules fit together;
- **pedagogical alignment**: the materials elicit and assess the intended reasoning;
- **artifact integrity**: the delivered files match the source and pass mechanical and visual checks; and
- **theory validation**: independent evidence supports the truth or empirical adequacy of Cø / N\*.

This report supports the first three. It does not purport to establish the fourth.

## 3. Findings and corrections

### A. Theory integration and unit of analysis

| Weakness identified | Correction made | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| The source profile $V\land N_1\land N_2\land N_3$ could be read as attributing consciousness to every all-pass container. | The manual now distinguishes causally admissible **test referents**, ontic bundle profiles, estimated profiles, the inclusion-minimal positive-bearer set, and a resolved-bearer license. | A larger all-pass system may contain a smaller positive bearer without itself being the bearer. |
| Requiring positive-bearer membership before component testing would make negative and single-failure audits circular. | Component and biconditional tests operate on stable test referents; positive subject attribution is reserved for the recomputed minimal-bearer output. | This permits genuine failed-component evidence without presupposing the positive result. |
| Plural minima, nested candidates, and content-indexed results could silently settle subject identity. | The manual adds separability, unresolved-plurality, joint-content, and cross-time mapping guardrails. | A content-level result does not automatically establish co-consciousness or diachronic personal identity. |
| The teaching synthesis could be mistaken for a quotation from the core paper. | It is explicitly labeled a **bearer-complete synthesis convention** and a proposed amendment needed to integrate the corpus. | Students can distinguish source commitments from a transparent instructor-level reconciliation. |

This synthesis is appropriate in a teacher's manual because it exposes, rather than hides, a tension among the papers. A future versioned theory note should state whether the research corpus formally adopts it.

### B. Target, evidence, and outcome discipline

The manual now consistently distinguishes minimal phenomenal presence from report, wakefulness, task access, attention, intelligence, metacognition, moral status, and phenomenal character. A study-level conclusion is indexed to a candidate, content, regime, grain, and interval; it is not automatically a claim of continuous or globally rich consciousness.

The four scientific statuses are operationalized separately:

- **positive:** all required lower bounds and licenses clear their frozen rules;
- **negative:** a licensed, direction-appropriate interval establishes decisive failure;
- **indeterminate:** the evidence is valid but does not resolve the frozen rule; and
- **unlicensed:** candidate identity, validity, transport, selectivity, independence, or another prerequisite fails.

Further corrections include:

- hatted estimates are separated from unhatted ontic properties;
- failure to establish a pass is not treated as ontic absence;
- equality and interval-straddling rules are declared explicitly;
- surrogate conflict is handled before scalar aggregation;
- equivalence requires the complete interval to fall inside the closed margin;
- negative rules are direction-specific for measures such as latency; and
- clinical, welfare, and precautionary action are analyzed separately from scientific classification.

### C. Boundary identification

The boundary workflow was strengthened from a suggestive score into a staged identification procedure:

1. discover candidate referents without inspecting the consciousness-linked outcome;
2. freeze candidate family, grain, regime, interval, mappings, and admissibility assumptions;
3. evaluate component roles on held-out evidence;
4. apply inclusion-minimality to the all-pass set;
5. test separability, mapping, replacement, and stability; and
6. report a unique bearer, separable plurality, unresolved plurality, negative result, indeterminate result, or unlicensed inference as appropriate.

The manual now requires intervention positivity/consistency assumptions, causal-model adequacy, matched internal and external intervention budgets, size or dimension normalization, measurement reliability, regime stability, and separate reporting of candidate reliability $R_C$ and bearer-output stability $R_B$. It also distinguishes same-specification multiplicity, cross-specification nesting, constituent support, interfaces, enabling support, and diachronic identity.

### D. Availability, recurrence, and component interpretation

The $N_2$ presentation now separates causal availability from activation, diffusion, report, executive access, node count, and fixed anatomy. The historical formulation is taught before the refined criterion, which adds content fidelity, class-native effects, latency, selectivity, route evidence, preparation burden, context coverage, recipient-class count, and evenness. The edge convention $H_A=0$ for fewer than two qualifying classes prevents an undefined entropy calculation.

The recurrence and viability treatments now distinguish bounded recurrent stabilization from mere duration, reverberation labels, pathological fixation, and feedforward persistence. Interventions must report retained-component equivalence, viability, candidate stability, and rescue logic rather than assuming that a named lesion selectively changes one conjunct.

### E. Ablation, differentiation, and validation

The manual previously risked treating a profile-level leave-one-out theorem as a theorem about final conscious bearers. It now distinguishes:

- logical inclusion in a conjunction;
- profile-level diagnostic disagreement;
- bearer-output disagreement after every rival is propagated through the frozen candidate lattice;
- incremental predictive value against a declared rival and independent anchor family; and
- selective causal contribution under intervention.

Criterion, mechanism, and measurement ablations are separated. Grouped ablations receive their own coverage and redundancy rules. A licensed mechanism intervention must move the target component by the minimum intended amount, preserve retained components within equivalence margins, preserve viability and stable referent identity, rule out relevant alternate routes, and recompute the bearer output rather than assuming it.

Validation is consequently presented as a program of risky tests:

- synthetic construct recovery;
- diagnostic near-miss coverage;
- selective perturbation and rescue;
- held-out transport;
- versioned rival prediction;
- independent implementation and readout; and
- prospective outcome-to-revision commitments.

Success at any one stage is explicitly prevented from becoming a claim of metaphysical identity or final theory confirmation.

### F. Character and schematic legibility

Phenomenal character is gated behind, but not reduced to, a positive presence classification. The bridge to relational phenomenal structure is independently tested; a failed bridge neither negates presence nor licenses denial of all character. Surviving automorphisms are reported as underdetermination rather than resolved by stipulation.

Schematic legibility is likewise modular. Decomposable semantics, compositional readout, fault localization, and evidential independence are assessed at the appropriate trial or dataset unit. Blind-reader agreement demonstrates public teachability under frozen rules, not the truth of the theory.

Every session contains collaborative application and discussion, not only lecture exposition. The repertoire includes jigsaws, structured academic controversy, reciprocal hearings, design studios, progressive-disclosure conferences, evidence tribunals, blind readout, transport audits, skeptical panels, and outcome-to-revision tribunals. Private commitment, rotating roles, minority notes, and concrete group artifacts reduce dominance and hindsight effects.

### H. Accessibility and assessment

The final source provides a session-specific accessible or digital alternative for all fourteen sessions. It avoids color-only encoding and offers linearized graphs, editable tables, read-aloud equations, written and asynchronous participation, quiet preparation, nonverbal response, remote participation, and flexible presentation roles. Assessment rewards target accuracy, inferential license, rival quality, revision, and documented dissent rather than agreement with the theory.

These design features are strong, but they are not a substitute for user testing. A representative screen-reader audit, table-navigation test, equation-reading test, contrast/reflow test, and classroom timing pilot remain recommended for the next release cycle.

### I. Audience safety and release engineering

The resource source now uses exact fail-closed audience blocks. The parser rejects unclassified substantive content, malformed or nested markers, duplicate IDs, missing keys or accessibility blocks, student leakage, instructor leakage into reveals, and any change to the audited 50-item reveal inventory or its order.

The original monolithic reveal artifact was unsafe for digital release. The build now creates:

- a key-free student base pack;
- a combined reveal master for instructor printing and editing; and
- **50 individually distributable, one-page reveal PDFs**, each with typed block ID, session, title, page count, and SHA-256 provenance.

Every reveal is matched to a PDF outline destination, split from the validated master, rescanned for another reveal title and instructor-only content, and recorded in the manifest. Instructors can therefore release one assigned item without exposing later evidence.

Publication is now transactional across the six master files, the reveal directory, and the manifest. Existing targets are retained as rollback copies until the complete replacement succeeds. A post-publication check rejects stale source hashes, mixed artifact hashes, missing or extra reveal files, and a manifest that does not describe the release on disk.

The repository README now links directly to the manual, student pack, individual reveal directory, build instructions, and this audit.

## 5. Residual limitations and recommendations

These are limitations of warrant or future quality improvements, not known release defects:

1. **The theory remains new and empirically open.** The manual's validation program specifies what would count for and against the model; it does not report that those tests have already succeeded.
2. **The bearer-complete synthesis should be versioned in the research corpus.** The manual labels it accurately, but a later paper or theory note should accept, revise, or reject the formulation explicitly.
3. **Independent anchors remain fallible and partly theory-laden.** Results must retain anchor genealogy, disagreement, held-out adjudication, and rival-relative scope.
4. **Thresholds and transport may be brittle.** Real studies need preregistered calibration, sensitivity neighborhoods, domain-shift tests, and reversal reporting.
5. **Synthetic classroom values are not empirical evidence.** They must not be reused for clinical, welfare, or artificial-consciousness judgments.
6. **Specialist accessibility validation remains desirable.** PDF tagging and alternative formats passed structural checks, but actual assistive-technology users should test representative prose, equations, tables, figures, and reveals.
7. **Pedagogical outcome validity remains unmeasured.** A pilot should collect completion times, error patterns, transfer performance, rubric agreement, and student accessibility feedback.
8. **External references can drift.** Bibliographic metadata, URLs, and rival-theory version cards should be rechecked when the corpus baseline changes.

## 6. Release verdict

**Approved for controlled graduate-level teaching distribution.**

No known blocking weakness remains in the audited source or generated teaching package. Distribution must preserve the audience policy: release the student base pack at course start; release only the assigned single-sheet PDF at each commitment point; retain the reveal master and instructor manual under instructor control.

This verdict concerns the manual's coherence, pedagogy, packaging, and artifact integrity. It is not a verdict that Cø / N\* is true, that its conjunction has been empirically validated, or that any target system is conscious.
